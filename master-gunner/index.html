Course Overview

What is Master Gunner?

The Master Gunner Course is a platform-specific advanced gunnery course available for Bradley, Abrams, and Stryker weapon systems. It produces unit gunnery experts who are responsible for managing weapons training programs, advising commanders on combined arms integration, and ensuring crews achieve and maintain combat-level proficiency.

Graduates serve as the commander's principal advisor on all matters related to gunnery training, weapons employment, and crew qualification. The Master Gunner is one of the most technically demanding positions an NCO can hold in the combat arms.

Course Phases

Building Gunnery Experts

  • Phase 1 Academic — Weapons systems theory, ballistics, fire control systems, ammunition types and effects, and platform-specific maintenance procedures. Heavy emphasis on understanding the technical fundamentals that drive gunnery performance.
  • Phase 2 Practical — Range operations, gunnery tables, training management, and qualification standards. Students plan and execute gunnery training, manage range operations, and demonstrate the ability to train and certify crews to standard.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Students must pass rigorous written examinations covering weapons systems theory, ballistics, and fire control. Practical gunnery exercises require students to demonstrate mastery of range operations and crew qualification procedures.

Graduates must prove their ability to train and qualify crews to standard, as well as design a comprehensive unit gunnery program from the ground up. The course demands both deep technical knowledge and the ability to translate that knowledge into effective training.
