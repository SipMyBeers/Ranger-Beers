Course Overview

What is the SHARP Career Course?

The SHARP Career Course develops full-time SHARP professionals who serve as brigade-level and above Sexual Harassment/Assault Response and Prevention specialists. Conducted at Fort Leavenworth, Kansas, the course produces certified professionals equipped to manage comprehensive SHARP programs.

Graduates serve in dedicated SHARP positions across the Army, providing victim advocacy, managing cases, advising commanders, analyzing program data, and developing prevention strategies. The course transforms selected NCOs and officers into subject matter experts on one of the Army's highest-priority programs.

Course Phases

Eight Weeks of Professional Development

  • Wks 1-4 Foundation Phase — SHARP policy and regulatory framework, victim advocacy principles, investigative procedures, case management systems, and trauma-informed care. Students build the foundational knowledge required for professional SHARP practice.
  • Wks 5-8 Advanced Phase — Program management, data analysis and reporting, training development, leadership advisory skills, and a capstone exercise. Students demonstrate their ability to manage a full SHARP program at the brigade level and above.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Students must pass written exams covering SHARP policy, victim advocacy, and investigative procedures. Case management scenarios test the ability to manage multiple cases simultaneously while maintaining proper documentation and victim support throughout the process.

Graduates must also deliver SHARP training to standard, pass a program management evaluation demonstrating their ability to run a brigade-level SHARP program, and meet all certification requirements established by the Army's SHARP program office.
