The Foundation phase establishes comprehensive knowledge of SHARP policy and prevention strategy. Students learn the regulatory framework, the Army's prevention model, and the critical distinctions between restricted and unrestricted reporting procedures.

Victim advocacy training introduces trauma-informed care principles that form the basis for all victim interactions. Students practice risk reduction assessment and learn to identify environmental and behavioral indicators of potential harm.

Bystander intervention training teaches practical techniques to safely and effectively intervene in situations before they escalate. This skill is foundational to the Army's prevention-first approach.

Foundation Topics
  • SHARP policy — regulatory framework, legal authorities, and Army directives
  • Prevention strategy — primary prevention model and risk factors
  • Reporting procedures — restricted vs. unrestricted, collateral reports
  • Victim advocacy — trauma-informed care, crisis response
  • Risk reduction — environmental assessment and mitigation planning
  • Bystander intervention — the 3D model (Direct, Distract, Delegate)
  • Command relationships — advising commanders on SHARP matters

Applied Skills is where knowledge becomes capability. Students face realistic scenario-based training that tests their ability to respond to SHARP incidents under pressure, manage competing priorities, and support victims through the reporting process.

Case studies drawn from real-world incidents teach critical decision-making in complex situations. Victim response simulations test crisis intervention skills with role players providing realistic victim reactions and emotional responses.

The capstone evaluation requires students to demonstrate mastery across all competencies — from initial victim contact through case management, commander advisory briefings, and unit-level prevention program design.

Applied Skills Events
  • Scenario-based training — multi-step incident response exercises
  • Case studies — analysis of real-world SHARP cases and outcomes
  • Victim response simulation — crisis intervention with role players
  • Briefing development — commander advisory and unit training briefs
  • Unit integration plan — designing a prevention program for your unit
  • Capstone evaluation — comprehensive practical assessment
  • Peer evaluation — leadership and communication skills review

Winter at Fort Leavenworth brings cold temperatures, but the SHARP course is entirely classroom-based. Temperatures range from 20–40°F with occasional snow and ice. Training is unaffected since all instruction takes place indoors.

Winter Tips
  • Dress in layers for commute to classroom facilities
  • Plan extra time for winter driving conditions on post
  • All training conducted in heated classrooms
  • Holiday block leave may affect December class schedules
  • Post dining and fitness facilities fully operational

Kansas summers are hot and humid, but all SHARP training takes place in air-conditioned classrooms. Temperatures routinely reach 90–95°F with high humidity. Plan morning PT early to avoid heat, and stay hydrated throughout the day.

Summer Tips

Don't Be That SHARP Student.

Every failure at the SHARP Course has a type. We built a fix for each one.

📜
Don't Know SHARP Policy?

The regulatory framework is dense and constantly evolving. If you can't cite AR 600-20 Chapter 7 and AR 600-20 Appendix H, you're not ready for the written exam.
😶
Afraid of Sensitive Topics?

SHARP professionals must discuss difficult subjects with confidence and empathy. If you can't lead hard conversations, you can't lead a prevention program.
📣
Weak Briefing Skills?

You'll brief commanders, units, and senior leaders. If your delivery is weak, your message won't land. Briefing development is a graded event.
🤝
Can't Handle Victim Response?

Victim response simulation is the hardest evaluation. If you freeze under emotional pressure or break protocol, you will fail the capstone.
🛡
No Prevention Strategy?

Prevention is the mission. If you can't design a unit-level prevention plan with measurable outcomes, you're missing the entire point of SHARP.
📝
Poor Report Writing?

Documentation saves cases and protects victims. Sloppy report writing undermines investigations and erodes trust in the SHARP program.
