What is the Army Space Badge?

The Army Space Badge was authorized in 2004 to recognize Soldiers who support the Army's growing space operations mission. Managed by U.S. Army Space and Missile Defense Command (SMDC), space operations support everything from satellite communications and GPS to missile warning and space-based intelligence.

The badge comes in three degrees: Basic (completion of a space operations course and 12 months in a space billet), Senior (completion of advanced space training and 36 months in space billets with additional qualifications), and Master (completion of advanced space education, 48+ months in space billets, and demonstrated expertise). Army space personnel serve at SMDC, Strategic Command, and combatant commands worldwide, providing critical space-enabled capabilities to tactical commanders.

Course Phases

Path to the Space Badge

  • 4-6 Wks Space Cadre Basic Course — Fundamentals of space operations, orbital mechanics, satellite communications, GPS operations, missile warning systems, space-based ISR, and space policy. Conducted at various locations including Ft Carson, CO and Peterson SFB.
  • Varies Space Operations Officer/NCO Course — MOS-specific training in space systems operations, satellite control, or missile defense. Advanced training in space threat environment and electromagnetic spectrum operations.
  • Progressive Advanced Space Education — Advanced Space Operations Course, Joint Space Operations certification, and specialized training in areas like satellite communications planning, missile defense, or space control operations.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Basic: Complete approved space operations course + 12 months serving in a validated Army space billet. Senior: 36+ months in space billets + completion of advanced space course + demonstrated proficiency. Master: 48+ months in space billets + advanced space education + demonstrated exceptional expertise and leadership in space operations.

All degrees require assignment to an Army space-coded position and commander recommendation.
