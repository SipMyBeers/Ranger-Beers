The Academics phase builds the doctrinal foundation for reconnaissance operations. Students learn the military decision-making process as it applies to recon planning, intelligence preparation of the battlefield, and the full range of reconnaissance and surveillance tasks at the company level and below.

Advanced land navigation is tested early — night land nav eliminates roughly 12% of the class. Students must demonstrate mastery of map reading, terrain association, and movement in restricted terrain before progressing to practical application.

Intelligence reporting formats are drilled repeatedly. Every observation must be converted into actionable intelligence using standardized formats that higher headquarters can process and act upon immediately.

Academic Topics
  • Reconnaissance planning using MDMP and TLP
  • Intelligence preparation of the battlefield (IPB)
  • Advanced day and night land navigation
  • SALUTE, SPOT, and INTREP reporting formats
  • Observation post selection and site planning
  • Target detection, identification, and range estimation
  • Communications planning for extended operations

Practical application is where classroom theory meets reality. Students build and occupy hide sites, establish observation posts, and conduct surveillance operations for extended periods while remaining undetected by opposing forces.

Sketching and reporting are graded events — panoramic sketches must meet exacting standards for detail, accuracy, and completeness. Every terrain feature, target, and observation must be documented in a format that provides actionable intelligence to supported commanders.

Students who cannot remain concealed, produce accurate reports, or maintain discipline during extended observation periods are dropped from the course.

Practical Events
  • Hide site construction and occupation
  • Observation post establishment and operations
  • Panoramic sketching and range card preparation
  • Extended surveillance site operations (24+ hours)
  • Reconnaissance patrol planning and execution
  • Tactical movement and infiltration techniques
  • Real-time intelligence reporting under pressure
FTX — Prove It in the Field
Week 3
Duration7 Days
LocationFort Liberty field training areas
FocusCulminating recon exercise
Requirements
Full Mission PlanningPlan recon from start to finish

The FTX integrates every reconnaissance skill into a sustained multi-day tactical operation. Students plan, brief, and execute full-spectrum reconnaissance and surveillance missions from infiltration through exfiltration under simulated combat conditions.

Leadership is evaluated throughout the FTX. Students must demonstrate they can plan reconnaissance operations, lead teams in the field, collect actionable intelligence, and report to higher headquarters — all while avoiding detection by opposing forces.

Failure to complete the FTX results in course failure regardless of previous performance. The FTX is the ultimate test of whether a student can function as a reconnaissance and surveillance leader.

FTX Requirements
  • Plan and brief a multi-day reconnaissance mission
  • Execute infiltration to objective area
  • Establish and operate surveillance sites
  • Collect and report intelligence to higher HQ
  • Lead reconnaissance teams under field conditions
  • Successfully exfiltrate without compromise

Winter RSLC classes test cold-weather discipline. Fort Liberty winters are mild compared to northern posts, but extended surveillance operations in freezing rain and near-freezing temps still cause significant cold-weather injuries. Long nights favor surveillance operations but make movement more demanding.

Winter Gear Tips
  • ECWCS layers — wet cold is worse than dry cold
  • Waterproof outer shell — rain and sleet are common
  • Insulated sleeping system for extended OP occupation
  • Extra socks and glove inserts — moisture management
  • Hand and toe warmers for long static positions

Spring is prime RSLC season. Moderate temperatures allow students to focus on skills rather than fighting the weather. Increasing vegetation provides natural concealment for hide sites and observation posts. Night temperatures remain cool enough for comfortable extended operations.

Spring Gear Tips
  • Lightweight layering system — temps swing widely
  • Permethrin-treated uniforms — tick season begins
  • Rain gear always — spring storms are sudden
  • Natural camouflage materials increasingly available
  • Lighter sleep system sufficient for most nights

Summer RSLC is a heat battle. North Carolina humidity makes extended field operations miserable. Thick vegetation provides excellent concealment but restricts observation. Insect pressure during static surveillance operations is relentless and tests mental discipline.

Summer Gear Tips
  • Hydration system — minimum 4 quarts for movement
  • Insect repellent and head net for OP operations
  • Permethrin-treated everything — chiggers are brutal
  • Lightweight, moisture-wicking base layers
  • Electrolyte supplements for extended field ops

Fall is an excellent RSLC window. Cooling temperatures make extended field operations manageable. Declining foliage reduces natural concealment, forcing students to rely more on fieldcraft skills for hide site construction. Longer nights favor surveillance operations.

Fall Gear Tips
  • Layerable system — mornings cold, afternoons warm
  • Rain gear — fall storms are common
  • Warmer sleep system for late-season classes
  • Extra camouflage materials — vegetation thinning
  • Mental preparation for longer, colder nights at OPs

Don't Be That RSLC Student.

Every failure at RSLC has a type. We built a fix for each one.

🗺
Bad at Recon Planning?

Reconnaissance planning isn't just an OPORD. It's MDMP applied to intelligence collection — and most students have never done it before. Learn the process now.
🌐
Can't Navigate at Night?

Night land navigation drops ~12% of the class. Terrain association, pace count, and dead reckoning in the dark separate recon leaders from everyone else.
👁
Weak Observation Skills?

If you can't detect, identify, and accurately report targets from an OP, you fail. Train your eye for detail, range estimation, and systematic scanning now.
📝
No Reporting Skills?

SALUTE reports, SPOT reports, and INTREPs must be accurate, timely, and actionable. Sloppy reporting means your intelligence is useless to the commander.
✏
Can't Sketch?

Panoramic sketches and range cards must be precise. If your sketch doesn't match reality, the supported unit can't use your intelligence. Practice drawing terrain features.
🌳
Poor Fieldcraft?

Hide site construction, camouflage discipline, and noise/light discipline determine whether you're detected. Get compromised and your mission — and grade — is over.
