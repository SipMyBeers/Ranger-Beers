Course Overview

What is USASS?

The United States Army Sniper School (USASS) is a seven-week course conducted at Fort Moore, Georgia, designed to produce highly skilled precision marksmen capable of delivering long-range fire, conducting surveillance, and collecting battlefield intelligence. The school trains soldiers in the art and science of sniping — combining marksmanship with field craft, camouflage, and mission planning.

Graduates return to their units as qualified snipers, capable of operating in two-man teams deep within hostile territory. The course is one of the most sought-after specialty schools in the Army, demanding exceptional patience, discipline, and shooting ability from every candidate.

Course Phases

7 Weeks of Precision

  • Wks 1-2 Marksmanship Fundamentals — Advanced rifle marksmanship, ballistics theory, scope employment, zeroing, and engagement techniques on known-distance ranges with the M24 and M110 weapon systems.
  • Wks 3-5 Field Craft — Stalking exercises, range estimation, target detection, concealment techniques, ghillie suit construction, observation and reporting, and hide-site operations.
  • Wks 6-7 Mission Planning & Execution — Full mission scenarios integrating all skills, final stalk evaluations, live-fire exercises, urban sniper operations, and graded practical assessments.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Students must qualify on both the M24 Sniper Weapon System and the M110 Semi-Automatic Sniper System, meeting strict accuracy standards at ranges out to 800 meters. Stalking exercises require soldiers to move undetected within close range of trained observer instructors and deliver a firing solution — the stalk pass rate is one of the course's highest attrition points.

Physical fitness standards must be maintained throughout the course, and candidates are evaluated on their ability to conduct detailed observation, produce range cards, and deliver accurate intelligence reports from concealed positions.
