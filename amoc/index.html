The Academics phase builds the doctrinal foundation for combined arms maneuver. Students work through the Military Decision-Making Process (MDMP) from mission analysis through orders production, learning to integrate fires, maneuver, and sustainment into a coherent operational plan.

Terrain analysis and Intelligence Preparation of the Battlefield (IPB) are taught at an advanced level. Students must demonstrate mastery of OAKOC and its application to course of action development before proceeding to the applied phase.

Fire support integration is a critical component — students learn to synchronize indirect fires, close air support, and electronic warfare with ground maneuver elements to create combined arms effects at the point of decision.

Academic Topics
  • Military Decision-Making Process (MDMP) — all seven steps
  • Course of action development and wargaming
  • Intelligence Preparation of the Battlefield (IPB)
  • Fire support planning and coordination measures
  • Combined arms integration at battalion and brigade echelons
  • Maneuver doctrine — ADP 3-90, FM 3-90-1, FM 3-90-2
  • Operations order (OPORD) format and production

Applied Operations is where students put doctrine into practice. Working in staff groups, students receive a tactical scenario and must produce a complete operations order integrating all warfighting functions under time pressure.

The operations brief is a high-stakes event — students present their plan to senior evaluators who challenge every assumption. Poor terrain analysis, weak fires integration, or an unsynchronized scheme of maneuver results in failure.

Situational training exercises force students to adapt their plans when the enemy doesn't cooperate. The final graded exercise requires students to demonstrate they can plan, brief, and execute a combined arms operation from start to finish.

Applied Operations Events
  • Receive tactical scenario and conduct mission analysis
  • Develop courses of action and conduct wargaming
  • Produce a complete five-paragraph OPORD
  • Conduct operations brief to senior evaluators
  • Execute combined arms situational training exercises
  • Adapt plans under changing battlefield conditions
  • Complete final graded combined arms planning exercise

Winter at Fort Moore is mild compared to northern installations. Classroom-heavy instruction means weather has minimal impact on training. Temperatures rarely drop below freezing, making any outdoor STX events comfortable.

Winter Tips
  • Lightweight cold-weather layers for outdoor events
  • Focus study time on MDMP and fires integration
  • Review ADP 3-90 and FM 3-90-1 before arrival
  • Prepare briefing skills — public speaking under pressure
  • Georgia winter is mild — focus on academics not weather

Spring is a popular time for AMOC classes. Pleasant weather at Fort Moore makes outdoor situational training exercises comfortable. Many units schedule leaders for spring classes to avoid summer training conflicts.

Spring Tips

Summer Tips

Don't Be That AMOC Student.

Every failure at AMOC has a type. We built a fix for each one.

📋
Weak MDMP?

The Military Decision-Making Process is the backbone of AMOC. If you can't execute all seven steps under time pressure, you will not pass the planning exercises.
💥
Bad Fires Integration?

Combined arms means integrating fires with maneuver. If your fire support plan is an afterthought, your scheme of maneuver falls apart under evaluation.
🌐
Poor Terrain Analysis?

OAKOC isn't optional. If you can't conduct a thorough terrain analysis and integrate it into your IPB, your entire plan will be built on a weak foundation.
📄
No OPORD Experience?

You must produce a complete five-paragraph operations order under time constraints. If you've never written one from scratch, start practicing now.
🎤
Weak Briefing Skills?

You will brief senior evaluators who will challenge your plan. Stumbling through a briefing or failing to defend your decisions is a fast path to a no-go.
🚗
Can't Plan Movement?

Tactical movement planning and route selection are critical. If you can't synchronize movement with fires and maneuver, your plan will not survive contact.
