Course Overview

What is AMOC?

The Air Movement Operations Course (AMOC) trains soldiers to plan and execute air movement operations using rotary and fixed-wing aircraft. Taught at Fort Lee, Virginia, the course produces qualified unit air movement planners who can coordinate the safe and efficient transport of personnel, equipment, and supplies by air.

AMOC graduates return to their units as subject matter experts capable of planning air movements from scratch, coordinating with aviation assets, and executing complex loading and sling-load operations in support of tactical missions.

Course Phases

Two Weeks of Air Movement Mastery

  • Week 1 Planning Phase — Load planning, aircraft characteristics and capabilities, documentation procedures, chalking procedures, and bump plan development. Students learn to calculate loads, prepare manifests, and plan for contingencies.
  • Week 2 Execution Phase — Sling-load operations, personnel loading procedures, mock-up training, and a full-scale air movement exercise. Students execute everything they planned in Week 1 under realistic field conditions.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Students must produce a complete air movement plan, conduct a sling-load inspection to standard, and execute personnel manifesting procedures. A written exam tests knowledge of aircraft characteristics, load planning, and air movement doctrine.

The course emphasizes practical application — students must demonstrate they can plan, brief, and execute an air movement operation from start to finish, including contingency procedures when plans change on the fly.
