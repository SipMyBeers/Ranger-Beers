Course Overview

What is MFF School?

The Military Freefall Parachutist Course (MFFPC) teaches Soldiers the techniques of freefall parachuting for tactical military operations. Conducted by the John F. Kennedy Special Warfare Center and School at Ft Liberty, North Carolina (ground training) and Yuma Proving Ground, Arizona (jump training), MFF School trains personnel to infiltrate by parachute from high altitudes (up to 25,000 feet).

Students learn HALO (High Altitude Low Opening) techniques — freefalling from altitude and opening at low altitudes to avoid detection — and HAHO (High Altitude High Opening) techniques — opening parachutes at high altitude and gliding long distances to the target. The course is primarily attended by Special Operations forces (Green Berets, Rangers, and PSYOP/CA personnel).

Course Phases

5 Weeks from Ground to Sky

  • Phase I Ground Phase (2 weeks, Ft Liberty) — Parachute rigging, aircraft procedures, body position, equipment familiarization, altitude chamber training, and emergency procedures. Wind tunnel training for body stabilization.
  • Phase II Jump Phase (3 weeks, Yuma PG) — Progressive freefall jumps from increasing altitudes. Daytime and nighttime jumps. Students perform combat equipment jumps with rucksack and weapon. Final jumps include HAHO navigation and HALO tactical insertions.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Students must be Airborne-qualified prior to attending MFF School. They must complete the minimum number of successful freefall jumps with increasing complexity, pass altitude chamber requirements, and demonstrate stable freefall body position and canopy control throughout the course.

The Military Freefall Parachutist Badge comes in two degrees: Basic (torch and dagger with parachute) and Master (star added, requires 75+ MFF jumps and jumpmaster qualification). Graduates leave with one of the most elite airborne capabilities in the U.S. military.
