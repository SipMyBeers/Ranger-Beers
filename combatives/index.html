Course Overview

What is MACP?

The Modern Army Combatives Program (MACP) is the U.S. Army's official hand-to-hand combat training system, built on a foundation of Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, wrestling, boxing, and other martial arts adapted for the battlefield. The program trains soldiers to fight and win in close-quarters situations where weapons may not be available or appropriate.

MACP progresses through four levels of increasing complexity, from basic ground-fighting techniques to advanced instructor certification. The program builds warrior ethos, mental toughness, and the confidence that comes from knowing you can handle yourself in any close-combat situation. Every MOS is eligible to train.

Course Phases

4 Levels of Combat

  • Level 1 Basic Combatives (40 hrs) — Clinch work, takedowns, achieving and maintaining dominant body position, basic submissions, and ground-fighting fundamentals. The foundation for all further combatives training.
  • Level 2 Tactical Combatives (40 hrs) — Striking techniques, stand-up fighting, weapons considerations in close quarters, fighting in kit, and integration of combatives with tactical scenarios.
  • Level 3 Advanced Combatives (40 hrs) — Fight strategy and tactics, coaching techniques, scenario-based training with protective equipment, and competitive fighting. Students must compete in a combatives tournament.
  • Level 4 Trainer/Instructor (80 hrs) — Teaching methodology, program management, competition rules and officiating, curriculum development, and certification to train and certify other soldiers at Levels 1-3.
Key Standards

What It Takes

At each level, candidates must demonstrate proficiency in all techniques taught during that phase and pass practical evaluations conducted by certified instructors. Level 1 and 2 focus on technical execution — can you perform the takedown, maintain the position, and apply the submission correctly under pressure?

Level 3 and above require competition experience, adding the stress of live fighting against resisting opponents to the evaluation criteria. Level 4 candidates must demonstrate the ability to teach, coach, and manage a unit combatives program, including organizing competitions and maintaining safety standards.
