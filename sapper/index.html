Course Overview

What is the Sapper Leader Course?

The Sapper Leader Course (SLC) is conducted at Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri, by the U.S. Army Engineer School. The 28-day course trains combat engineers in advanced demolitions, breaching operations, and tactical leadership under extreme physical and mental stress. Graduates are awarded the Sapper Tab — one of only four permanent skill tabs authorized by the Army.

SLC is one of the most physically and mentally demanding courses in the U.S. Army. It transforms experienced engineers into elite combat leaders capable of planning and executing complex breaching, demolition, and tactical operations. The course emphasizes mastery of engineer fundamentals under sleep deprivation, caloric deficit, and continuous stress.

Course Phases

28 Days to the Sapper Tab

  • Days 1–3 Assessment & Selection — APFT (minimum 60 per event, age-adjusted), 12-mile ruck march in under 3 hours, swim assessment, obstacle course, and Sapper physical fitness assessment. Eliminates ~20% of candidates.
  • Days 4–10 General Engineering Phase — Demolitions calculations and live charges, mine warfare, urban breaching (mechanical, ballistic, explosive, manual), bridging operations, and route clearance procedures.
  • Days 11–17 Tactical Phase — Small-unit tactics, reconnaissance, ambushes and raids with engineer integration. Students plan and execute missions incorporating obstacle construction, reduction, and combined-arms breaching.
  • Days 18–26 Patrol Phase (FTX) — Extended field training exercise combining all engineer and tactical skills. Multi-day operations with minimal sleep, carrying combat loads while performing complex engineer tasks. Peer evaluations.
  • Days 27–28 Final Assessment & Graduation — End-of-course evaluations, equipment turn-in, after-action reviews, and the tabbing ceremony.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Candidates must score a minimum of 60 points per event on the APFT (age-adjusted), complete a 12-mile ruck march under 3 hours with a 35-lb load, pass the combat water survival test, and navigate the obstacle course. Students must demonstrate mastery of demolitions math, breaching techniques, and tactical planning throughout the course.

The physical and mental demands are extreme. Students typically lose 15–20 pounds during the 28-day course due to caloric deficit and continuous operations. Only about 35% of candidates who begin the course earn the Sapper Tab.
