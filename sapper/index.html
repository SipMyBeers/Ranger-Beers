Course Overview

What is the Sapper Leader Course?

The Sapper Leader Course (SLC) is a 28-day course at the U.S. Army Engineer School, Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri. It is the Army's premier combat engineer leadership course, designed to produce technically and tactically proficient combat engineer leaders. The course is open to all combat engineers (MOS 12-series) and select combat arms soldiers.

Graduates earn the "SAPPER" tab, worn on the left shoulder. SLC is widely considered one of the Army's most physically and mentally demanding courses, with an attrition rate often exceeding 60%. The course tests expertise in demolitions, urban breaching, route clearance, bridging, and combined-arms operations.

Course Phases

28 Days of Engineer Excellence

  • Days 1-3 Assessment & Selection — APFT (minimum 60 per event, age-adjusted), 12-mile ruck march in under 3 hours, swim assessment, obstacle course, and Sapper physical fitness assessment. This phase eliminates roughly 20% of candidates.
  • Days 4-10 General Engineering Phase — Demolitions calculations and live charges, mine warfare, urban breaching (mechanical, ballistic, explosive, manual), bridging operations, and route clearance procedures. Heavy focus on hands-on engineer tasks under time pressure.
  • Days 11-17 Tactical Phase — Small-unit tactics, reconnaissance, ambushes and raids with engineer integration. Students plan and execute missions incorporating obstacle construction, reduction, and combined-arms breaching. Graded leadership positions.
  • Days 18-26 Patrol Phase (FTX) — Extended field training exercise combining all engineer and tactical skills. Students execute multi-day operations with minimal sleep, carrying combat loads while performing complex engineer tasks. Peer evaluations and graded patrols.
  • Days 27-28 Final Assessment & Graduation — End-of-course evaluations, equipment turn-in, after-action reviews, and the tabbing ceremony for graduates who have met all standards.
Key Standards

What It Takes

The Sapper Leader Course requires candidates to pass a demanding physical assessment up front: APFT minimum 60 per event, 12-mile ruck march under 3 hours with a 35-pound load, combat water survival test, and an obstacle course. Throughout the course, students must demonstrate mastery of demolitions math, breaching techniques, and tactical planning.

The combined physical demands, sleep deprivation, and technical complexity make SLC one of the Army's most challenging leadership courses. Students typically lose 15-20 pounds during the 28 days, and the course demands both physical grit and engineer-level problem-solving skills under extreme stress.
