EIB Standards

Physical requirements, testing standards, and grading criteria for the Expert Infantry Badge.

Physical Fitness

APFT Requirements

Candidates must pass the Army Physical Fitness Test (APFT) on the extended scale, scoring a minimum of 80% in each event. This is a gate event — failure on any single APFT event eliminates the candidate from further testing.

Testing Requirements

What You Must Demonstrate

  • MOS 11-series (Infantry) or 18-series (Special Forces) qualification
  • APFT score of 80% or higher on the extended scale in each event
  • Written examination on infantry doctrine, weapons systems, and tactical procedures
  • GO on all hands-on warrior task lanes: weapons, land nav, first aid, comms, call for fire
  • GO on patrol lane evaluation: tactical movement, battle drills, and leadership
  • Complete 12-mile ruck march with 35-lb minimum load in under 3 hours
Grading

How You're Evaluated

EIB uses a strict GO/NO-GO grading system for all hands-on warrior tasks. There is zero tolerance for error on most task lanes — a single critical step missed results in a NO-GO. Candidates are typically allowed only one retry across all lanes; a second NO-GO results in elimination.

The written exam requires a passing score, and the APFT demands 80% on the extended scale per event. The 12-mile ruck march is the final gate: candidates must complete it within 3 hours carrying a minimum 35-pound load. The combination of zero-deficiency standards and cumulative pressure is what drives the roughly 20% graduation rate.
