Course Overview

What is Drill Sergeant Academy?

The Drill Sergeant Academy transforms experienced NCOs into Drill Sergeants at Fort Jackson, South Carolina. Graduates go on to lead Initial Entry Training (IET), where they are directly responsible for shaping raw civilians into disciplined, physically fit, and tactically proficient Soldiers.

Serving as a Drill Sergeant is one of the Army's most prestigious duties. The iconic campaign hat, or "brown round," represents the authority, discipline, and commitment required to forge the next generation of the Army's fighting force.

Course Phases

Nine Weeks to the Brown Round

  • Wks 1-3 Academic Phase — Army regulations, training doctrine, counseling techniques, physical readiness training (PRT), and drill and ceremony mastery. Candidates learn to teach, coach, and mentor while maintaining the highest standards of discipline.
  • Wks 4-6 Practical Phase — Conduct BCT training events, evaluate performance, lead PT sessions, and manage IET soldiers. Candidates practice running training lanes, delivering classes, and handling the daily challenges of leading new recruits.
  • Wks 7-9 Evaluation Phase — Graded practicals, peer evaluations, a final practical exercise, and a board appearance before the commandant. This phase determines who has earned the right to wear the Drill Sergeant identification badge.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Candidates must meet strict physical fitness standards, demonstrate mastery of drill and ceremony, pass teaching evaluations, and score well on comprehensive written exams. Every aspect of a candidate's bearing, knowledge, and leadership is evaluated throughout the course.

The culminating event is the commandant's board — a formal evaluation where candidates must demonstrate their fitness, military knowledge, teaching ability, and readiness to assume the responsibilities of a Drill Sergeant.
