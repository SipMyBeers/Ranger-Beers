Course Overview

What is the CIB?

The CIB was established during WWII (1943) to recognize the unique role of infantry Soldiers who bear the primary burden of close combat. It is awarded to Soldiers holding infantry or SF MOS (11/18-series) who have been personally present and under hostile fire while serving in an assigned infantry/SF unit of brigade size or smaller.

The CIB is one of the most revered awards in the U.S. Army — it signifies that a Soldier has "been there" and endured the realities of ground combat. Second and third awards are denoted by superimposed stars.

Award Criteria

Steps to the Badge

  • Step 1 Eligibility — Must hold an 11-series (Infantry) or 18-series (Special Forces) Military Occupational Specialty.
  • Step 2 Combat Action — Must be personally present and under hostile fire in a ground combat engagement against the enemy.
  • Step 3 Unit Requirement — Must be assigned to an infantry or Special Forces unit of brigade size or smaller while operating in an active combat zone.
  • Step 4 Award Processing — Commander recommendation through the chain of command, verified against orders and combat action reports.
Key Standards

What It Takes

The CIB cannot be awarded to non-infantry Soldiers regardless of combat action — those Soldiers receive the CAB instead. Only one CIB may be awarded per qualifying war or conflict. It is retroactive to 6 December 1941.

The badge is a silver and enamel musket on a blue background with an oak wreath. Second and third awards are distinguished by superimposed stars on the wreath, marking additional qualifying conflicts.
