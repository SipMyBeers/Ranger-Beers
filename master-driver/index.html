Course Overview

What is the Master Driver Course?

The Master Driver Trainer Course trains NCOs to serve as their unit's Master Driver Trainer, the designated authority responsible for all vehicle licensing, driver training programs, and compliance with Army motor vehicle regulations. Every unit that operates military vehicles requires a qualified Master Driver Trainer.

Graduates return to their units equipped to manage the entire driver training program — from initial licensing through advanced vehicle operations. They ensure soldiers are properly trained and licensed on all assigned vehicles, reducing accidents and maintaining operational readiness across the fleet.

Course Phases

2 Weeks of Training

  • Week 1 Classroom Phase — Army regulations (AR 600-55), licensing procedures, training management systems, record keeping, accident reporting and prevention, and unit driver training program requirements.
  • Week 2 Practical Phase — Hands-on vehicle operation, administering licensing road tests, teaching methodology for driver training, night driving operations, convoy procedures, and practical examinations.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Candidates must pass a comprehensive written examination covering Army motor vehicle regulations, licensing procedures, and training management. The exam tests knowledge of AR 600-55, risk management frameworks, and the administrative requirements for maintaining a unit driver training program.

The practical driving evaluation assesses each candidate's ability to safely operate military vehicles and, critically, their ability to evaluate other soldiers and conduct effective driver training. Graduates must demonstrate they can build and manage a complete unit licensing program from the ground up.
