The Classroom phase covers Army motor vehicle regulations and administrative procedures. Students learn the regulatory framework governing vehicle operations, starting with AR 600-55 (The Army Driver and Operator Standardization Program) and the responsibilities of the Master Driver/Trainer.

Licensing procedures are a major focus — students master the OF 346 (Motor Vehicle Operator's Permit), dispatch operations, and the administrative requirements for maintaining a unit-level driver training program. Record keeping, accident reporting, and composite risk management complete the week.

A comprehensive written examination at the end of Week 1 tests knowledge across all regulatory and administrative topics. Students must pass this exam before advancing to the Practical phase.

Classroom Topics
  • AR 600-55 — Army Driver and Operator Standardization Program
  • Licensing procedures — OF 346, dispatch, and record keeping
  • Unit driver training program development and management
  • Accident reporting — DA Form 285, investigation procedures
  • Composite risk management for vehicle operations
  • Vehicle maintenance management — PMCS and TM procedures
  • Defensive driving principles and safety standards

The Practical phase puts students behind the wheel and in front of the class. Hands-on vehicle operation across wheeled and tracked platforms ensures every graduate can demonstrate proficiency before teaching others.

Teaching methodology is the real test. Students must prove they can evaluate other soldiers, administer licensing road tests, and conduct effective driver training. Night driving operations under blackout and NVG conditions are evaluated. Convoy planning and execution rounds out the week.

The practical examination determines whether a student can build and manage a complete unit licensing program from the ground up. Graduates leave certified to run their unit's entire driver training operation.

Practical Training Events
  • Hands-on vehicle operation — wheeled and tracked platforms
  • Administering licensing road tests — evaluator methodology
  • Teaching methodology — conducting effective driver training
  • Night driving operations — blackout and NVG conditions
  • Convoy planning, briefing, and execution
  • PMCS execution and maintenance management
  • Practical examination — complete driver program demonstration

Don't Be That Master Driver Student.

Every failure at the Master Driver Course has a type. We built a fix for each one.

🗣
Bad at Teaching Driving?

You're not just a driver — you're a trainer. If you can't break down vehicle operations into teachable steps and evaluate soldiers, you'll fail the practical exam.
📄
Don't Know Licensing?

OF 346, DA Form 5984, dispatch procedures — the licensing paperwork is where most students stumble. Learn the forms before you arrive.
🔧
Weak on Maintenance?

PMCS isn't optional. A Master Driver who can't enforce maintenance standards or read a -10 TM is a liability to the unit's entire fleet readiness.
🚚
No Convoy Ops?

Convoy planning, march orders, and movement control are on the practical exam. If you've never organized a convoy, start studying now.
📖
Can't Read TMs?

Technical manuals are the backbone of Army maintenance. If you can't navigate a -10 or -20 level TM, your PMCS program will fail and vehicles will break.
⚠
Poor Safety Record?

Accident reporting, risk management, and safety enforcement are core Master Driver duties. The course tests your ability to prevent mishaps, not just react to them.
