Course Overview

What is Tomb Guard Duty?

The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier Guard Identification Badge is the least-awarded badge in the U.S. Army — rarer than any special skill badge in the inventory. Soldiers assigned to the 3rd U.S. Infantry Regiment "The Old Guard" at Ft Myer, Virginia volunteer for Tomb Guard duty and undergo one of the most demanding training and selection processes in the military.

Guards walk the mat in front of the Tomb 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, in all weather conditions, executing a precisely choreographed sentinel's walk — 21 steps, 21-second pause, turn and repeat. The training period is approximately 9 months of intensive preparation before a Soldier can earn the coveted Tomb Guard Identification Badge. Only about 20% of volunteers earn the badge, and fewer than 700 badges have been awarded since 1958.

Course Phases

Nine Months to the Badge

  • Months 1-3 New Soldier Training — Physical training, military history, precision drill, uniform preparation, and memorization of extensive knowledge including the history of the Tomb, Arlington National Cemetery, and every Medal of Honor recipient buried there.
  • Months 4-6 Tomb Guard Training — Walking the mat under supervision, mastering the sentinel's walk (21 steps, exactly 90 feet), perfecting the "changing of the guard" ceremony, and demonstrating flawless weapon handling. All movements must be precise to fractions of an inch.
  • Months 7-9 Testing & Qualification — Written exam (must score 95%+), uniform inspection (must be flawless), practical evaluation walking the mat, and final board review. Multiple evaluations before badge is awarded.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Soldiers must maintain immaculate physical appearance — uniforms are prepared with hours of work for each shift. Guards must memorize extensive historical knowledge and recite it on demand. The sentinel's walk must be executed with mechanical precision.

Physical fitness standards are among the highest in the Army. Tomb Guards voluntarily agree to restrictions on their personal conduct for life (or until they surrender the badge). The badge features a gold-colored depiction of the Tomb, an inverted wreath, and the Greek figure of Grief.
