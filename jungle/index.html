Course Overview

What is JOTC?

The Jungle Operations Training Center (JOTC) is a U.S. Army school operated by the 25th Infantry Division at Schofield Barracks and the surrounding jungle training areas on Oahu, Hawaii. JOTC teaches small-unit tactics, survival skills, and waterborne operations in one of the most demanding jungle environments available to the U.S. military.

Graduates earn the coveted "JUNGLE" tab, which is worn on the left shoulder of the Army combat uniform. The course is open to all branches and allied forces, and has been training warriors in jungle warfare since the Vietnam era.

Course Phases

21 Days in the Jungle

  • Days 1-3 In-Processing & Assessment — APFT, swim assessment, equipment issue, and orientation to the jungle environment. Covers basic jungle movement techniques and acclimatization.
  • Days 4-10 Jungle Skills Phase — Jungle survival skills including shelter construction, water procurement, fire craft, land navigation under canopy, knot tying, rappelling, and rope bridges. Introduction to jungle-specific patrolling.
  • Days 11-14 Waterborne Operations — Stream and river crossings, zodiac boat operations, small-boat movements, waterfall rappels, and amphibious insertion techniques.
  • Days 15-19 Patrol Phase (FTX) — Extended field training exercise. Students plan and execute jungle patrols including ambushes, raids, reconnaissance, and react-to-contact drills in dense vegetation. Graded leadership positions.
  • Days 20-21 Final Assessment & Graduation — End-of-course evaluations, equipment turn-in, after-action reviews, and the tabbing ceremony for graduates.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Candidates must pass the APFT with a minimum score of 60 points per event, complete a 100-meter swim in uniform, and pass a water survival assessment. During the course, students must demonstrate proficiency in jungle navigation, survival tasks, waterborne operations, and small-unit tactics. Patrol phase requires passing a graded leadership position.

The jungle environment itself is the biggest challenge — oppressive heat and humidity, constant rain, insects, limited visibility, and difficult terrain test every student physically and mentally.
