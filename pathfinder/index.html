Week 1 covers the academic and practical foundations of Pathfinder operations. Students learn to select, survey, and establish drop zones (DZ) and landing zones (LZ) for airborne and air assault operations. Navigation calculations — including magnetic declination, grid-magnetic angle, and distance/azimuth conversions — must be precise.

A decimal error in any calculation means failure. The written exam is a major elimination point — students must score 70% or higher to continue. DZ/LZ survey calculations are graded with zero tolerance for mathematical mistakes.

This is the phase that sets the tone for the entire course. It is heavily academic, and students who arrive without strong math fundamentals and map-reading skills face an uphill battle from day one.

Week 1 Training
  • DZ/LZ selection and survey — field practical evaluations
  • Navigation calculations — magnetic declination, grid-magnetic angle
  • Distance and azimuth conversions — precision graded
  • DZ/LZ marking systems — day and night configurations
  • GMRS/VIRS equipment operation and setup
  • Written exam — 70% minimum, major elimination point
  • Grid-magnetic angle conversions — zero tolerance for errors

Week 2 focuses on helicopter sling-load operations — the same critical skill tested at Air Assault School, but at a deeper level. Students learn to rig, inspect, and certify loads for helicopter external lift using various sling configurations (single-point, two-point, four-point).

Written and practical evaluations test precise knowledge of load weights, center of gravity, and rigging procedures. Every knot, every strap, every connection point must be correct. A single rigging error during the practical evaluation means failure.

Students work with CH-47 Chinook and UH-60 Black Hawk external lift configurations. The sling-load written exam covers load classification, rigging procedures, and safety protocols in detail.

Week 2 Training
  • Sling-load rigging — single-point, two-point, four-point configurations
  • Load certification — weight calculations and center of gravity
  • Helicopter hookup procedures — ground guide and hookup team
  • CH-47 Chinook external lift — tandem and center hook
  • UH-60 Black Hawk external lift — cargo hook operations
  • Sling-load written exam — 70% minimum
  • Sling-load practical evaluation — hands-on rigging graded

The final week covers tactical Air Traffic Control (ATC) procedures — directing fixed-wing and rotary-wing aircraft into austere landing zones using verbal commands and visual signals. Precise phraseology and confident execution are non-negotiable.

The culminating FTX requires students to establish and operate a full pathfinder set — DZ/LZ survey, marking, ATC, and sling-load operations — under simulated tactical conditions. This is where everything comes together. Every skill from Weeks 1 and 2 must be executed under pressure.

Successful completion of the FTX and all evaluations earns the Pathfinder Torch — one of the most respected badges in Army aviation and airborne operations. Graduation typically occurs on Friday of Week 3.

Week 3 Training
  • Tactical ATC procedures — fixed-wing and rotary-wing control
  • Verbal and visual aircraft control — precise phraseology
  • VIRS/GMRS operation — equipment setup and calibration
  • Night operations — limited visibility ATC and DZ/LZ marking
  • Culminating FTX — establish full pathfinder set under tactical conditions
  • Graduation — Pathfinder Torch pinning ceremony
