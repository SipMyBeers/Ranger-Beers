Course Overview

What is the CMB?

The CMB was established in 1945 to recognize medical personnel who provide medical care under hostile fire. It parallels the CIB for medical Soldiers, acknowledging that medics routinely expose themselves to extreme danger while treating casualties on the battlefield.

The CMB is awarded to medical department Soldiers (MOS 68W Combat Medic or assigned to a medical role) who are personally present and under fire while performing medical duties. Like the CIB, it is one of the most respected awards in the Army. Second and third awards are marked with superimposed stars.

Award Criteria

Steps to the Badge

  • Step 1 Eligibility — Must be a medical department Soldier, typically holding MOS 68W (Combat Medic) or serving as a medical officer.
  • Step 2 Combat Action — Must provide medical care while personally present and under hostile fire in a ground combat engagement.
  • Step 3 Unit Requirement — Must be attached or assigned to an infantry or Special Forces unit, or otherwise engaged in ground combat operations.
  • Step 4 Award Processing — Commander recommendation supported by medical treatment records, after-action reports, and witness statements.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Only medical personnel performing medical duties qualify — a medic carrying a rifle but not treating casualties does not meet the criteria. The Soldier must be actively providing medical care during the combat engagement to be eligible.

The badge features a stretcher bearing cross on a blue field with an oak wreath. Only one CMB may be awarded per qualifying conflict. Second and third awards are distinguished by superimposed stars on the wreath.
