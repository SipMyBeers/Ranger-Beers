The Academics phase builds the regulatory and systems foundation every Career Counselor needs. Students master Army retention regulations, reenlistment eligibility criteria, and the full range of incentive and bonus programs available to Soldiers.

Students learn to navigate the RETAIN system, iPERMS, and the Army Career Tracker — the digital tools that drive modern retention operations. Understanding these systems is non-negotiable for processing reenlistments and managing unit retention data.

Academic exams test knowledge of AR 601-280, bonus programs, reclassification procedures, and career management systems. Failure to pass written exams results in course removal.

Academic Topics
  • AR 601-280 — Army Retention Program regulations
  • Reenlistment eligibility, processing, and bar to reenlistment procedures
  • Selective Reenlistment Bonus (SRB) and incentive management
  • Reclassification, In/Out calls, and MOS management
  • Education benefits — Tuition Assistance, GI Bill, credentialing
  • RETAIN system operations and retention reporting
  • Career development counseling frameworks

The Practicum phase is where book knowledge meets execution. Students conduct live counseling sessions, delivering career guidance to role-players with realistic Soldier scenarios — from first-term privates weighing reenlistment to senior NCOs considering retirement.

Retention briefings are a core competency. Students must brief commanders and formations on retention programs, bonus changes, and career opportunities. Poor briefing skills are a fast path to failure.

The capstone event is developing a comprehensive unit retention program. Students build a complete plan covering outreach, counseling schedules, reenlistment goals, and reporting requirements — the same deliverable expected on day one at their gaining unit.

Practicum Events
  • One-on-one career counseling sessions with graded scenarios
  • Commander retention briefing — prepare and deliver
  • Formation retention briefing — benefits and programs overview
  • Mock reenlistment ceremony planning and execution
  • Unit retention program development and presentation
  • RETAIN system practical exercises — live data entry
  • Bar to reenlistment processing and appeals

Fort Knox is the home of Army retention training. The Army Retention School conducts the Career Counselor Course year-round, producing qualified 79S Career Counselors for units across the force. The installation provides classroom facilities, computer labs for RETAIN system training, and practicum environments.

Installation Tips
  • Arrive with a working CAC and updated ORB/ERB
  • Bring regulation references — AR 601-280 at minimum
  • Laptop recommended for after-hours study
  • Professional dress standards — ASU for reenlistment ceremonies
  • Louisville, KY is 30 minutes north for weekend amenities

Don't Be That Career Counselor Student.

Every failure at the Career Counselor Course has a type. We built a fix for each one.

💬
Bad at Counseling?

One-on-one career counseling is a graded event. If you can't build rapport, read body language, and guide a Soldier to a decision, you'll fail the practicum.
📖
Don't Know Programs?

SRBs, CSRBs, reclassification, education benefits, credentialing — Soldiers expect you to know every program cold. Study before you arrive.
📣
Weak Briefing Skills?

You'll brief commanders, formations, and individual Soldiers. If you freeze at the podium or can't articulate programs clearly, the course will expose it.
📜
Can't Read Regulations?

AR 601-280 is the bible. If you can't parse regulatory language, cross-reference policy, and apply it to real scenarios, academics will bury you.
🤝
No People Skills?

Career Counselors live and die by interpersonal skills. Every day is face-to-face with Soldiers at career crossroads. Empathy isn't optional — it's the job.
💻
Poor Data Entry?

RETAIN, iPERMS, and Army Career Tracker are your daily tools. Sloppy data entry means lost bonuses, missed reenlistments, and angry commanders.
