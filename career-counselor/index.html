Course Overview

What is the Career Counselor Course?

The Army Career Counselor Course trains selected NCOs to serve as unit-level retention and career advisors. Conducted at Ft Jackson, South Carolina, the course produces qualified Career Counselors (79S) who advise commanders on retention programs and counsel individual Soldiers on reenlistment options, reclassification, bonus programs, and career development.

Career Counselors are critical to Army readiness — they ensure the Army retains its best talent. The Career Counselor wears a distinctive identification badge and serves as the commander's primary advisor on all retention matters. Career Counselors must understand the full range of Army career options, benefits, and incentive programs.

Course Phases

Eight Weeks to the Badge

  • Wks 1-5 Academic Phase — Army retention regulations, reenlistment procedures, career management systems, bonus programs, reclassification procedures, education benefits, and counseling techniques. Students learn the Army Career Tracker system and retention reporting requirements.
  • Wks 6-8 Practical Phase — Hands-on counseling practicum, mock reenlistment ceremonies, career briefings, retention interview techniques, and unit retention program development. Students conduct actual counseling sessions and develop retention plans.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Must pass academic exams on retention regulations, career management procedures, and Army benefits programs. Must demonstrate effective counseling skills during practical evaluations.

Students must develop a comprehensive unit retention plan. Career Counselors earn the Career Counselor Identification Badge upon course completion and assignment to a career counselor position.
