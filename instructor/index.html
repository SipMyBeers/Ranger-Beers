The Methodology phase builds the instructional foundation every Army instructor needs. Students learn adult learning theory, how to write terminal and enabling learning objectives, and how to structure a lesson plan that achieves measurable outcomes.

Training aid development and media integration are introduced — effective instruction demands more than talking. Students learn to design visual aids, handouts, and multimedia content that reinforce key learning points and keep students engaged.

Assessment design teaches instructors to build written and performance evaluations that accurately measure student comprehension. By the end of Week 1, every student has a complete lesson plan ready for platform delivery.

Methodology Topics
  • Adult learning theory — principles, motivation, retention
  • Learning objectives — terminal, enabling, and action-condition-standard
  • Lesson plan structure — introduction, body, summary, assessment
  • Training aid development — slides, handouts, props
  • Assessment construction — test items, rubrics, performance checklists
  • Training management — scheduling, resources, coordination
  • Communication fundamentals — clarity, projection, confidence

Platform Instruction is where theory meets reality. Students stand in front of a classroom and teach — multiple times, with increasing complexity and scrutiny. Nervousness is expected. Improvement is required.

Each practice teach is followed by structured peer and cadre feedback. Students learn to give and receive constructive criticism — a skill as important as teaching itself. Video review helps students see their own mannerisms, filler words, and body language.

The final graded evaluation requires a 45-minute lesson delivered to standard. Students must demonstrate command presence, proper use of training aids, effective questioning techniques, and accurate assessment of student learning.

Platform Events
  • Practice teach 1 — 20-minute lesson with peer feedback
  • Practice teach 2 — 30-minute lesson with cadre evaluation
  • Final graded teach — 45-minute comprehensive lesson
  • Peer critique sessions — structured feedback methodology
  • Video self-review — identifying strengths and areas for improvement
  • Questioning techniques — engaging students and checking comprehension
  • Classroom management — handling disruptions, maintaining pace

Winter classes are less frequent due to holiday block leave schedules across TRADOC installations. January and February classes tend to be smaller, which can mean more individualized cadre feedback during practice teaches.

Winter Tips
  • Use holiday leave to finalize lesson plan drafts
  • Smaller class size means more platform time per student
  • Coordinate early — slots fill fast after the new fiscal year
  • Review TRADOC Reg 350-70 over the break
  • Practice presentations with family during the holidays

Spring is the busiest season for SGITC. TRADOC installations run multiple classes as units push to fill instructor billets before the summer PCS cycle. Expect full classrooms and a competitive atmosphere.

Spring Tips
  • Register early — spring slots fill months in advance
  • Larger classes mean more diverse peer feedback
  • Networking opportunity with instructors across branches
  • Arrive with lesson plan topics pre-selected
  • Coordinate TDY orders well ahead of report date

Summer brings PCS-driven demand. Soldiers arriving at new instructor assignments need certification quickly. Some installations run accelerated schedules to certify incoming instructors before the fall training cycle begins.

Summer Tips
  • Ideal timing if you just PCS'd to an instructor billet
  • Coordinate with gaining unit for immediate enrollment
  • Some installations offer condensed summer schedules
  • Use the transition period to observe experienced instructors
  • Prepare lesson plans on your new subject matter area

Fall kicks off the new fiscal year. Fresh TDY budgets mean units can fund instructor certification early. October and November classes are typically well-attended as commands prioritize filling instructor shortages before the holiday season.

Fall Tips
  • New FY funding — best time to get TDY orders approved
  • Get certified before holiday block leave begins
  • Plan to start instructing immediately upon return
  • Coordinate with S-3 for annual training calendar alignment
  • Fall graduates can begin teaching in the spring cycle

Don't Be That Instructor.

Every failure at SGITC has a type. We built a fix for each one.

😬
Afraid of Public Speaking?

Stage fright is the number one obstacle. If standing in front of 30 soldiers makes your mouth go dry, you need reps — not courage. Start practicing now.
📝
Bad Lesson Plans?

A weak lesson plan is a weak class. If your objectives are vague, your structure is unclear, or your transitions are missing, the cadre will notice immediately.
😴
Can't Engage Students?

Reading slides to a room of bored soldiers is not instruction. Questioning techniques, active participation, and energy management separate instructors from readers.
📈
Weak Assessment Skills?

If you can't measure learning, you can't prove teaching happened. Building valid test items and performance checklists is a science, not a guess.
🖼
No Training Aids?

A blank whiteboard and a monotone voice will fail every evaluation. Training aids — slides, props, handouts, demonstrations — are required, not optional.
⏰
Poor Time Management?

Running 10 minutes over or finishing 15 minutes early both fail the standard. Pacing a 45-minute block requires rehearsal, not winging it.
