Overview

What You'll Do

As a Special Forces Medical Sergeant (18D), you are the medical expert on the ODA. You'll provide advanced trauma care, minor surgery, and long-term medical treatment in austere environments, training indigenous forces in medical tactics and healthcare.

Career Phases

PHASE 1
SF Pipeline

Candidates must first pass Special Forces Assessment and Selection (SFAS). Following selection, they begin the Special Forces Qualification Course (SFQC) with small unit tactics and language training.

PHASE 2
Medical Training

Special Operations Combat Medic (SOCM) course and the SF Medical Sergeant Course at Fort Liberty. 36-50 weeks of intense medical training including trauma, surgery, and clinical medicine.

PHASE 3
Operational Force

Assigned to a Special Forces Group. Deploying globally on 12-man ODAs. Responsible for the health and welfare of the team and host-nation partner forces.

PHASE 4
Strategic Leader

Transitioning to Team Sergeant or Operations Sergeant. Managing large-scale medical networks and advising senior commanders on medical strategy.
