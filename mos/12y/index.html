Overview

What You'll Do

As a Geospatial Engineer (12Y), you collect, analyze, and distribute geospatial information to support tactical and operational planning. You'll create precise maps, terrain models, and geographic databases to ensure the force has the best information possible.

Key Responsibilities

  • Collect and analyze geospatial data from diverse sources
  • Create precise maps, charts, and terrain models
  • Manage geographic information systems (GIS) and databases
  • Provide terrain analysis support to commanders
  • Distribute geospatial products to the force

Training

Basic Combat Training followed by Advanced Individual Training at Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri. Specialized training in GIS, mapping, and terrain analysis.
