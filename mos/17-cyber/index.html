Career Field Overview

What is Army Cyber?

The Cyber branch was established in 2014 as the Army's newest branch, reflecting the growing importance of cyberspace as a warfighting domain. Cyber Operations Specialists (17C) perform offensive and defensive cyber operations, including penetration testing, vulnerability assessment, incident response, and network exploitation. Electronic Warfare Specialists (17E) conduct electromagnetic spectrum operations to deny, degrade, or exploit adversary communications and radar systems.

Training is conducted at Ft Eisenhower (formerly Ft Gordon), Georgia, home of the Army Cyber Center of Excellence. The Cyber branch has some of the longest AIT pipelines in the Army due to the technical complexity of the field. Graduates emerge with skills comparable to civilian cybersecurity professionals and hold industry-recognized certifications. Cyber Soldiers serve in Cyber Protection Teams, Cyber Mission Forces, and at U.S. Cyber Command.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Cyber Operator

  • Step 1BCT — Complete Basic Combat Training before entering one of the Army's most technically demanding AIT programs.
  • Step 224-52 Week AIT at Ft Eisenhower, GA — One of the longest AIT programs in the Army. Covers networking, operating systems, programming, exploitation techniques, and defensive operations. Includes hands-on cyber range exercises.
  • Step 3Unit Assignment — Report to a Cyber Protection Team, Expeditionary Cyber Team, or unit at Ft Eisenhower, Ft Meade, or other locations. Begin real-world cyber operations.
  • Step 4Industry Certifications — Earn CompTIA Network+, Security+, CEH, OSCP, and other certifications. Cyber Soldiers are expected to continually develop their technical skills.
  • Step 5NCOES & Advanced Cyber Ops — Progress through NCOES while pursuing SANS courses, Offensive Cyber Operations training, Joint Cyber Analysis courses, and specialized Red/Blue team assignments.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Cyber Soldiers can pursue: SANS/GIAC certifications, Offensive Cyber Operations Course, Joint Cyber Analysis Course, CompTIA Network+, Security+, CEH (Certified Ethical Hacker), OSCP (Offensive Security Certified Professional), and advanced Red Team/Blue Team training at various Department of Defense cyber ranges.
