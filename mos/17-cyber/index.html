Career Field at a Glance

24-52 Week AIT — 2 MOS — 1 Mission: Cyber Warfare

The 17-Series is the Army's digital warfighting force. From offensive network exploitation to electromagnetic spectrum dominance, Cyber Soldiers defend networks and project power where bytes are bullets.

MOS Specialties
17C
Cyber Operations Specialist
AIT52 Weeks
LocationFort Eisenhower, GA
ASVABST: 112
ClearanceTS/SCI
Key SkillsNetwork Exploitation, Pen Testing, Malware Analysis, Vulnerability Assessment

The 17C Cyber Operations Specialist conducts offensive and defensive cyber operations across the full spectrum of cyberspace. With a 52-week AIT at Fort Eisenhower — one of the longest in the Army — 17Cs emerge as highly trained cyber warriors capable of network exploitation, vulnerability analysis, malware analysis, and penetration testing.

Training covers networking fundamentals, operating systems (Windows/Linux), programming and scripting, exploitation techniques, defensive operations, and incident response. Graduates earn industry-recognized certifications and serve on Cyber Protection Teams, Cyber Mission Forces, and at U.S. Cyber Command.

  • Offensive cyberspace operations and network exploitation
  • Vulnerability analysis and penetration testing
  • Malware analysis, reverse engineering, and forensics
  • Defensive cyber operations and incident response
  • Programming and scripting (Python, PowerShell, Bash)
  • Network architecture, protocols, and traffic analysis
  • Hands-on cyber range exercises with live-fire scenarios
17E
Electronic Warfare Specialist
AIT24 Weeks
LocationFort Eisenhower, GA
ASVABST: 112
ClearanceTS/SCI
Key SkillsElectronic Attack, Electronic Protection, SIGINT Integration, Spectrum Management

The 17E Electronic Warfare Specialist conducts electromagnetic spectrum operations to deny, degrade, disrupt, or exploit adversary communications and radar systems. 17Es are the Army's experts in electronic attack (EA), electronic protection (EP), and electronic warfare support (ES) — controlling the invisible battlespace that modern warfare depends on.

AIT at Fort Eisenhower covers electromagnetic spectrum theory, EW equipment operation, SIGINT integration, and spectrum management. 17Es serve at every echelon from battalion to theater, integrating electronic warfare into combined arms operations.

  • Electronic attack: jamming, spoofing, and denial operations
  • Electronic protection: hardening friendly systems and ECCM
  • Electronic warfare support: spectrum monitoring and threat detection
  • SIGINT integration and signals analysis
  • Electromagnetic spectrum management and deconfliction
  • EW equipment operation, maintenance, and troubleshooting
  • Integration with maneuver, fires, and intelligence operations
17Z
Senior Cyber Operations Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE / CYBERCOM
DutySenior Enlisted Leader
Key SkillsCyber Ops Planning, Team Leadership, Mission Management

The 17Z Senior Cyber Operations Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted Cyber leadership. 17Zs serve as operations sergeants major, first sergeants, and command sergeants major for Cyber units at battalion, brigade, and CYBERCOM level. They are responsible for cyber operations planning, team leadership, mission management, and the technical and tactical readiness of Cyber forces.

Reaching 17Z requires years of proven cyber operations experience, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a demonstrated ability to lead in one of the Army's most technically complex fields.

  • Cyber operations planning and mission management
  • Cyber Protection Team and Cyber Mission Force leadership
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career development
  • Technical readiness oversight and training management
  • Cross-domain integration with intelligence and signal operations
  • Mentorship of junior NCOs and cyber workforce development
  • Strategic-level cyber operations at CYBERCOM and above
Career Progression
E1-E4
Junior Enlisted
Timeline0-4 Years

CertsSecurity+, Net+, CEH
Pay$24K-$36K/yr
BonusUp to $50K (initial)

Junior enlisted Cyber Soldiers begin their career on Cyber Protection Teams or in support roles at cyber units, applying the skills learned during their intensive AIT. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years, bringing increased responsibilities on operational teams and mission sets.

This is the time to earn industry certifications, build technical depth, and establish a reputation for competence in one of the Army's most intellectually demanding fields. Cyber MOS qualifies soldiers for some of the highest enlistment bonuses in the Army.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Junior operator, basic cyber tasks
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient team member, certification pursuit
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Experienced operator, mentor to new arrivals
  • Pursue CompTIA Security+, Network+, and CEH certifications
  • Participate in cyber competitions and capture-the-flag events
  • Build technical portfolio and pursue advanced training opportunities
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsTeam Lead, Section Lead
SchoolsBLC, ALC, SANS/GIAC
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER + Certifications

Cyber NCOs lead teams of 3-6 operators on Cyber Protection Teams and Cyber Mission Forces. Sergeants (E-5) lead work roles within a team, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) lead entire sections. This is where technical expertise meets leadership — directly responsible for mission execution and the training of junior cyber operators.

Completion of BLC is required for E-5, and ALC for E-6. SANS/GIAC certifications, OSCP, and advanced cyber training significantly improve promotion potential and assignment options.

  • SGT (E-5): Team Lead — leads 3-4 operators in cyber mission execution
  • SSG (E-6): Section Lead — leads sections, responsible for all training
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • SANS/GIAC certifications are the gold standard for Cyber NCOs
  • Pursue OSCP, GPEN, GCIH, and advanced Red/Blue team roles
  • Strong NCOER evaluations and technical competence drive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsOps NCOIC, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior Cyber NCOs represent the most experienced and capable leaders in the Cyber branch. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as Operations NCOICs and team senior enlisted, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and CYBERCOM.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of successful cyber operations, advanced certifications, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential for selection.

  • SFC (E-7): Operations NCOIC — senior tactical NCO for cyber teams
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations SGM or First Sergeant — company-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE/CYBERCOM senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Instructor and joint cyber billets build competitive records
17A
Cyber Officer / Direct Commission
PathDirect Commission / OCS
DurationVaries by pathway
LocationFort Eisenhower, GA
Result17A Commission
UniqueDirect commission possible

The Cyber branch is one of the few Army branches that offers a direct commission pathway for experienced civilian cybersecurity professionals. Candidates with industry experience, advanced certifications, and relevant degrees can bypass traditional OCS and commission directly as Cyber Officers (17A). Traditional OCS and ROTC commissioning paths are also available.

Prior enlisted Cyber Soldiers who commission bring invaluable operational experience. Cyber Officers lead Cyber Mission Forces, plan and execute cyberspace operations, and integrate cyber effects into combined arms operations at every echelon.

  • Direct Commission: experienced cyber professionals bypass traditional commissioning
  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — traditional commissioning path
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • Cyber Basic Officer Leader Course (CBOLC) follows commissioning
  • Prior enlisted experience is a significant advantage in team leadership
  • Career progression: PL → XO → CO CDR → Cyber BDE Staff → BN CDR

Common Questions
Career
Want to Hack for the Army?

17C Cyber Operations Specialists conduct offensive cyber operations including penetration testing, network exploitation, and vulnerability assessment. It's the closest thing to legal hacking in uniform.

Certifications
Need Security+ Cert?

CompTIA Security+ is a baseline requirement for most Cyber positions. The Army funds certification training during AIT and throughout your career. Most 17Cs earn it before graduating AIT.

Training
Struggling with Networking?

Networking is the foundation of everything in Cyber. Master TCP/IP, subnetting, routing, and network architecture. If you can't understand the network, you can't defend or exploit it.

Schools
How Long is Cyber AIT?

17C AIT is 52 weeks — one of the longest in the Army. 17E AIT is 24 weeks. Both are conducted at Fort Eisenhower, GA. The length reflects the technical complexity of the field.

ASVAB
What's the ASVAB Score?

Cyber requires an ST score of 112 — the highest ASVAB requirement in the Army. This reflects the advanced technical aptitude needed for cyber operations.

Transition
Civilian Cyber Career?

Cyber Soldiers are among the most sought-after veterans in the civilian workforce. Skills in penetration testing, incident response, and network defense translate directly to six-figure cybersecurity careers.
