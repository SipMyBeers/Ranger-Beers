Career Field Overview

What are Engineers?

The Army Engineer branch is one of the most versatile in the military. Combat Engineers (Sappers) lead the way on the battlefield by breaching obstacles, clearing routes, constructing fighting positions, and emplacing or removing explosive hazards. They operate alongside Infantry and Armor units in direct combat, ensuring freedom of maneuver for friendly forces while denying it to the enemy. The Engineer motto, "Essayons," reflects their willingness to tackle any challenge.

Beyond combat engineering, the branch encompasses general engineering specialties responsible for horizontal and vertical construction, bridging, plumbing, electrical systems, and terrain analysis. Engineers build everything from forward operating bases to roads, airfields, and bridges in austere environments. The career field offers diverse opportunities ranging from demolitions and route clearance to underwater construction and heavy equipment operations.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Engineer

  • Step 1 14-Week OSUT/AIT at Ft Leonard Wood, MO — Combat Engineers attend One Station Unit Training, while other 12-series MOS attend BCT followed by AIT. Training covers demolitions, mine warfare, construction fundamentals, and combat engineering tasks.
  • Step 2 First Unit Assignment — Report to an Engineer battalion or brigade and begin integrating into your unit. Gain hands-on experience with explosives, heavy equipment, or construction operations depending on your MOS.
  • Step 3 Sapper Leader Course — The premier leadership course for Engineers. An intense 28-day course covering advanced demolitions, reconnaissance, urban breaching, and mountaineering. Graduates earn the Sapper tab.
  • Step 4 NCOES (BLC / ALC / SLC) — Progress through the Noncommissioned Officer Education System as you advance in rank. Each course builds leadership, technical skills, and tactical proficiency.
  • Step 5 Advanced Schools — Senior Engineers pursue specialized training such as Ranger School, Airborne, advanced demolitions courses, or route clearance qualifications.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Engineers have access to some of the most technically demanding courses in the Army. Available advanced training includes: Sapper Leader Course, Ranger School, Airborne School, Advanced Demolitions Training, Route Clearance Course, and Dive School.
