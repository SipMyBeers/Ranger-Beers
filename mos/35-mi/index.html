Junior enlisted MI Soldiers begin their career in their assigned intelligence discipline, working under the supervision of experienced NCOs in tactical or strategic intelligence environments. Obtaining and maintaining a TS/SCI clearance is the immediate priority after AIT graduation.

This is the time to build technical proficiency in your MOS, pursue additional schools, and develop the analytical foundation for future NCO leadership in intelligence operations.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Learn systems, support analysis production
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient analyst/collector, beginning independent work
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Experienced intelligence professional, mentor to new arrivals
  • Pursue Airborne, Air Assault for SOF support assignments
  • Earn certifications in intelligence systems and platforms
  • Build technical expertise for competitive promotion and school selection

Noncommissioned Officer

SchoolsBLC, ALC, AMIC
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER performance

MI NCOs lead intelligence sections and teams, directly supervising intelligence production and collection operations. Sergeants (E-5) lead small analytical or collection teams, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) manage larger sections and serve as shift leaders in intelligence operations centers.

Completion of BLC is required for E-5, and ALC for E-6. Advanced MI courses and joint intelligence training significantly improve promotion potential and open doors to special operations support and agency assignments.

  • SGT (E-5): Section Leader — supervises 3-5 intelligence professionals
  • SSG (E-6): Team Chief — manages intelligence production for a section or shift
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • Advanced MI courses at Fort Huachuca enhance technical expertise
  • Pursue joint assignments with DIA, NSA, or SOF units
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsS-2 NCOIC, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior MI NCOs represent the most experienced intelligence leaders in the enlisted force. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as intelligence section NCOICs, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level or serve as S-2 NCOICs at battalion, and Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at brigade and higher.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of excellence across multiple intelligence assignments, advanced training, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential for selection.

  • SFC (E-7): Intelligence Section NCOIC — senior tactical MI NCO
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): S-2 NCOIC or First Sergeant — company/battalion-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BDE/MI BN senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Joint and agency assignments build competitive records

BonusBranch selection

Enlisted MI Soldiers with leadership potential can pursue commissioning through OCS, Green-to-Gold, or the Warrant Officer path. MI Warrant Officers (350-series) are technical intelligence experts who provide continuity and deep expertise in intelligence operations. Commissioned officers lead intelligence organizations at platoon through brigade level.

Former enlisted MI soldiers who commission bring invaluable technical intelligence experience that sets them apart. Many of the Army's best S-2s and intelligence company commanders came up through the enlisted MI ranks.

  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — rapid commissioning path
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • Warrant Officer: Technical intelligence expert track (350F, 350G, etc.)
  • MI Basic Officer Leader Course (MIBOLC) at Fort Huachuca follows commissioning
  • Prior enlisted MI experience is a massive advantage in intelligence leadership
  • Career progression: PL → XO → CO CDR → BN/BDE S-2 → BN CDR

Common Questions
Career
Want TS/SCI Clearance?

Nearly every 35-Series MOS requires a Top Secret/SCI clearance. The investigation process begins during AIT and can take 6-12 months. A clean background and financial responsibility are essential.

Training
Struggling with Analysis?

Intelligence analysis is a learned skill. Master the IPB process, practice structured analytical techniques, and learn to write concise intelligence products. Critical thinking is your most important weapon.

Language
Need Language Skills?

35P Cryptologic Linguists attend the Defense Language Institute in Monterey, CA. Language categories range from 36 to 64 weeks. DLAB scores determine which languages you qualify for.

Schools
How Long is 35P AIT?

35P has the longest training pipeline in the MI career field — 36-52+ weeks between DLI language training and Goodfellow AFB SIGINT school. Plan for a year or more in training status.

MOS
HUMINT vs SIGINT?

HUMINT (35M) collects intelligence through people — interrogation, source operations, tactical questioning. SIGINT (35P/35N) collects through electronic signals. Both are critical and highly transferable to civilian careers.

Transition
Civilian Intel Career?

MI experience and a TS/SCI clearance are among the most valuable assets in the civilian job market. Three-letter agencies, defense contractors, and tech companies actively recruit former 35-Series soldiers.

Key Takeaway

Military Intelligence is one of the most intellectually demanding and career-transferable MOS fields in the Army. If you want to provide commanders with the intelligence overmatch that wins battles, earn a TS/SCI clearance, and build skills that translate directly to civilian intelligence and tech careers, the 35-Series is the path. Study hard, maintain a clean record, and stay curious.
