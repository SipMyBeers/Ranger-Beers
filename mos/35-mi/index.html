Career Field Overview

Knowledge Is the Edge

Military Intelligence soldiers are the eyes and ears of the Army. The 35-Series covers an enormous range of disciplines — from all-source intelligence analysis and geospatial imagery to human intelligence collection, signals intelligence, counterintelligence, and cyber operations. MI professionals operate at every echelon, from tactical battalions to strategic-level agencies, providing commanders with the intelligence they need to make informed decisions.

The MI career field is one of the most diverse in the Army, with AIT lengths ranging from 13 weeks to over a year depending on the specialty. Many MOS require Top Secret/SCI security clearances, and several include language training at the Defense Language Institute. MI soldiers regularly work alongside special operations forces, joint agencies, and the broader intelligence community, making it one of the most impactful and transferable career fields in the military.

Training Pipeline

Building Intelligence Professionals

  • Phase 1 Basic Combat Training — 10 weeks of BCT at one of the Army's training installations covering warrior tasks, physical fitness, and soldiering fundamentals.
  • Phase 2 13-52 Week AIT — Advanced Individual Training at Ft Huachuca, AZ (or DLI Monterey for linguists). Length varies dramatically by MOS, with linguists attending the longest programs.
  • Phase 3 Security Clearance — Obtain and maintain a Top Secret/SCI security clearance, which is required for nearly all 35-Series positions.
  • Phase 4 Unit Assignment — Report to your first operational unit and begin applying intelligence skills in a real-world environment at tactical, operational, or strategic echelons.
  • Phase 5 Certifications & NCOES — Earn professional certifications, attend NCOES for career progression, and develop expertise in your intelligence discipline.
  • Phase 6 Advanced Assignments — Opportunities for joint assignments, special operations support, agency billets, and advanced intelligence training.
Advanced Training

Specialized Courses

MI soldiers have access to an extensive array of advanced training: HUMINT Collector Course, SIGINT Analysis certification, Counterintelligence Agent Course, Imagery Analysis qualification, Language School at the Defense Language Institute (DLI) in Monterey, CA, and Joint Intelligence courses at national-level agencies. Many MI professionals also attend airborne, air assault, or Ranger School to support special operations intelligence requirements.
