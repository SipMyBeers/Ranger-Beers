Career Field at a Glance

22-Week OSUT — 3 MOS — 1 Mission: Close With & Destroy

The 11-Series is the Army's premier ground combat force. From light infantry to mechanized warfare, Infantry Soldiers own the fight at the point of contact.

MOS Specialties
11B
Infantryman
OSUT22 Weeks
LocationFort Moore, GA
ASVABCO: 87
DutyCombat Arms
Key SkillsMarksmanship, Land Nav, Patrolling, Battle Drills, CQB

The 11B Infantryman is the foundation of the Army's ground combat capability. Every infantry platoon, company, and battalion depends on 11Bs to close with and destroy the enemy through fire and maneuver. OSUT at Fort Moore combines Basic Combat Training and Advanced Individual Training into a continuous 22-week course that transforms civilians into lethal, disciplined Infantry Soldiers.

Training progresses from individual warrior tasks through team and squad-level operations, culminating in company-level field training exercises. Graduates are qualified in M4/M249/M240 weapon systems, land navigation, combat patrolling, urban operations, and casualty evacuation.

  • Advanced marksmanship with M4A1, M249 SAW, M240B, AT-4, and M320
  • Land navigation (day/night, mounted/dismounted)
  • React to contact, ambush, and IED battle drills
  • Squad and platoon patrolling operations
  • Urban operations and close quarters battle (CQB)
  • Casualty evacuation (CASEVAC) and tactical combat casualty care
  • Field training exercise (FTX) with live-fire lanes
11C
Indirect Fire Infantryman
OSUT22 Weeks
LocationFort Moore, GA
ASVABCO: 87
DutyCombat Arms
Key SkillsMortar Gunnery, FDC, Fire Missions, Ballistics

The 11C Indirect Fire Infantryman operates the 60mm, 81mm, and 120mm mortar systems organic to infantry battalions. Mortarmen provide the commander's most responsive indirect fire capability — steel on target within minutes of a call for fire. 11Cs complete the same 22-week OSUT as 11Bs, with additional specialized mortar training in the final weeks.

11Cs must master fire direction center (FDC) operations, mortar gunnery tables, ballistic computations, and the ability to deliver accurate fire in all conditions. They serve in mortar sections at company and battalion level.

  • 60mm, 81mm, and 120mm mortar system operation and maintenance
  • Fire Direction Center (FDC) procedures and digital fire control
  • Manual and computerized gunnery computations
  • Call for fire procedures and fire mission processing
  • Mortar ballistics, charge selection, and safety T&E
  • Displacement, emplacement, and survivability operations
  • Integration with company and battalion maneuver plans
11Z
Infantry Senior Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE Level
DutySenior Enlisted Leader
Key SkillsTraining Mgmt, Operations, Doctrine, Mentorship

The 11Z Infantry Senior Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted Infantry leadership. 11Zs serve as operations sergeants major, first sergeants, and command sergeants major at battalion and brigade level. They are responsible for training management, personnel readiness, and tactical employment of the unit's Infantry force.

Reaching 11Z requires decades of Infantry experience, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a record of combat leadership and proven performance across multiple duty positions.

  • Training management and readiness reporting at BN/BDE level
  • Operations planning and orders production
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Doctrine development and tactical innovation
  • Unit-level resource and logistics management
  • Mentorship of junior NCOs and officer integration
  • Combat leadership at the highest enlisted levels
Career Progression
E1-E4
Junior Enlisted
Timeline0-4 Years
PositionsRifleman, TL, Driver
SchoolsAirborne, Air Assault
Pay$24K-$36K/yr
BonusUp to $50K (initial)

Junior enlisted Infantry Soldiers begin their career as riflemen in a fire team, learning squad-level tactics, weapons proficiency, and field craft. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years and brings increased responsibility as a team member or vehicle driver.

This is the time to pursue additional schools and build the foundation for NCO leadership. Infantry MOS qualifies soldiers for some of the Army's most sought-after training opportunities.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Rifleman, basic soldier tasks
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient team member, weapons qualification expert
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Experienced soldier, mentor to new arrivals
  • Pursue Airborne, Air Assault, Ranger School early
  • Volunteer for deployment and special duty assignments
  • Build PT score for competitive promotion and school selection
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsTeam Leader, Squad Leader
SchoolsBLC, ALC, Ranger
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER performance

The NCO corps is the backbone of the Infantry. Sergeants (E-5) lead fire teams of 3-4 Soldiers, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) lead squads of 9-12. This is where Infantry leaders are forged — directly responsible for the training, discipline, and tactical employment of soldiers in combat.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Ranger School and other advanced training significantly improve promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Team Leader — leads 3-4 soldiers in fire team operations
  • SSG (E-6): Squad Leader — leads 9-12 soldiers, responsible for all training
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • Ranger School is the premier leadership school for Infantry NCOs
  • Pursue Jumpmaster, Pathfinder, Sniper, or RSLC
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsPSG, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior NCOs represent the most experienced and capable leaders in the Infantry. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as Platoon Sergeants, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and higher.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of combat leadership, advanced school completion (especially Ranger), and consistent top-block evaluations are essential.

  • SFC (E-7): Platoon Sergeant — senior tactical NCO for 30-40 soldiers
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — company-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Instructor and drill sergeant duty build competitive records
OCS
Officer Commissioning
PathOCS / Green-to-Gold
Duration12-16 Weeks (OCS)
LocationFort Moore, GA
Result2LT Commission
BonusBranch selection

Enlisted Infantry Soldiers with leadership potential and a bachelor's degree (or willingness to earn one) can pursue commissioning through Officer Candidate School (OCS) or the Green-to-Gold program. OCS is a 12-week intensive course at Fort Moore, while Green-to-Gold sends soldiers to college full-time with an ROTC scholarship.

Former enlisted soldiers who commission as Infantry Officers bring invaluable tactical experience that sets them apart from their peers. Many of the Army's best platoon leaders and company commanders came up through the enlisted ranks.

  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — rapid commissioning path
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • Infantry Basic Officer Leader Course (IBOLC) follows commissioning
  • Ranger School is effectively required for Infantry Officers
  • Prior enlisted experience is a massive advantage in platoon leadership
  • Career progression: PL → XO → CO CDR → BN/BDE Staff → BN CDR

Common Questions
Career
Want to Go Airborne?

Infantry Soldiers have priority for Airborne School at Fort Moore. Three weeks of ground, tower, and jump training earns you silver wings and opens the door to the 82nd Airborne and Special Operations.

Training
Struggling with Land Nav?

Land navigation is a make-or-break skill in Infantry. Master terrain association, pace count, and night movement. Practice with a compass and protractor until it's second nature.

Fitness
Need Better PT Scores?

Physical fitness defines Infantry culture. A strong ACFT score (500+) opens doors to every school in the Army. Focus on deadlift, sprint-drag-carry, and the 2-mile run.

Schools
Eyeing Ranger School?

Ranger School is the gold standard for Infantry leaders. Start preparing Day 1 — ruck, patrol, and starve. The Ranger tab is the most respected symbol in the Infantry.

MOS
11B or 11C?

Both share the same OSUT foundation. 11C mortarmen add indirect fire expertise and FDC operations. 11Bs focus on direct-action combat skills. Both are lethal.

Promotion
How Fast Can I Promote?

Infantry promotion to E-5 averages 3-4 years. BLC completion, a strong NCOER, Ranger tab, and airborne/air assault wings dramatically accelerate the timeline.
