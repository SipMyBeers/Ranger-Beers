Career Field at a Glance

14-Week OSUT — 3+ MOS — 1 Mission: Fire Support

The 13-Series is the Army's premier indirect fire force. From cannon sections to digital fire control, Field Artillery Soldiers deliver precision fires that shape the battlefield before ground forces close with the enemy.

MOS Specialties
13B
Cannon Crewmember
OSUT14 Weeks
LocationFort Sill, OK
ASVABFA: 93
DutyCombat Arms
Key SkillsM777, M109 Paladin, Fire Missions, Ammo Handling

The 13B Cannon Crewmember is the backbone of Field Artillery. Cannon crews operate the M777A2 towed howitzer and M109A7 Paladin self-propelled howitzer, delivering indirect fire in support of maneuver forces. OSUT at Fort Sill combines Basic Combat Training and Advanced Individual Training into a continuous 14-week course that transforms civilians into qualified artillerymen.

Training progresses from individual soldier tasks through section-level gunnery operations, culminating in live-fire exercises. Graduates are qualified in howitzer operations, ammunition handling, fire mission processing, and section displacement. Senior 13Bs progress to Section Chief, responsible for an entire howitzer section.

  • M777A2 towed howitzer and M109A7 Paladin operation and maintenance
  • Fire mission execution from call for fire to rounds downrange
  • Ammunition identification, handling, fuzing, and preparation
  • Howitzer emplacement, boresighting, and displacement operations
  • Section Chief duties: crew drill, safety, and mission supervision
  • Night fire missions and degraded-mode (manual) gunnery
  • Live-fire qualification with high-explosive and illumination rounds
13F
Fire Support Specialist
AIT12 Weeks
LocationFort Sill, OK
ASVABFA: 93
DutyCombat Arms
Key SkillsCall for Fire, FIST, JFO, Targeting

The 13F Fire Support Specialist is the eyes of the artillery on the front lines. Embedded with maneuver units as part of a Fire Support Team (FIST), 13Fs call for and adjust indirect fire, coordinate close air support, and integrate joint fires into the ground commander's scheme of maneuver. They operate forward with the infantry, often under direct enemy fire.

13Fs must master call-for-fire procedures, digital fire control systems, target location using DAGR/PLGR and laser rangefinders, and Joint Fires Observer (JFO) qualification. They serve as the critical link between the maneuver force and every fire support asset on the battlefield.

  • Call for fire procedures and fire mission adjustment
  • FIST operations and integration with maneuver companies
  • Joint Fires Observer (JFO) qualification and certification
  • Target location, designation, and battle damage assessment
  • Digital fire control systems and communications equipment
  • Close air support (CAS) request and coordination procedures
  • Fire support planning and target list development
13J
Fire Control Specialist
AIT9 Weeks
LocationFort Sill, OK
ASVABFA: 93
DutyCombat Arms
Key SkillsAFATDS, Fire Direction, Tactical Data

The 13J Fire Control Specialist operates the Advanced Field Artillery Tactical Data System (AFATDS) and other digital fire control systems that form the nerve center of artillery operations. Working in the Fire Direction Center (FDC), 13Js process fire missions, compute firing data, and manage the tactical data flow that ensures rounds hit their targets.

13Js must master AFATDS operations, manual gunnery computations as a backup, and the integration of multiple fire support systems. They are the brains behind every fire mission, translating observer requests into precise firing data for the gun line.

  • AFATDS operation, configuration, and troubleshooting
  • Fire direction procedures and mission processing
  • Tactical data system management and network operations
  • Manual gunnery computation (degraded-mode backup)
  • Fire support coordination and deconfliction
  • Mission tracking, reporting, and battle damage assessment
  • Integration with battalion and brigade fire support networks
13Z
Senior Field Artillery Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE Level
DutySenior Enlisted Leader
Key SkillsFA Operations, Training Mgmt, Doctrine

The 13Z Senior Field Artillery Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted FA leadership. 13Zs serve as operations sergeants major, first sergeants, and command sergeants major at battalion and brigade level. They are responsible for training management, personnel readiness, and the tactical employment of the unit's artillery force.

Reaching 13Z requires decades of Field Artillery experience, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a record of proven performance across cannon, fire support, and fire control positions.

  • BN/BDE-level FA operations planning and execution
  • Training management and gunnery qualification oversight
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Fire support doctrine development and tactical innovation
  • Unit-level resource and logistics management
  • Mentorship of junior NCOs and officer integration
  • Combat leadership at the highest enlisted levels
Career Progression
E1-E4
Junior Enlisted
Timeline0-4 Years
PositionsCannoneer, Ammo Bearer, Driver
SchoolsAirborne, Air Assault
Pay$24K-$36K/yr
BonusUp to $40K (initial)

Junior enlisted Field Artillery Soldiers begin as cannoneers on a howitzer section, ammunition bearers, or vehicle drivers. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years and brings increased responsibility as a gunner or assistant section chief.

This is the time to master the fundamentals of gunnery, pursue additional schools, and build the foundation for NCO leadership. Strong performance on section certifications and live-fire exercises sets the stage for competitive promotion.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Cannoneer, basic crew duties
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient crew member, weapons qualification expert
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Gunner or assistant section chief, mentor to new arrivals
  • Pursue Airborne, Air Assault, or Ranger School early
  • Master section certification and live-fire gunnery tables
  • Build PT score for competitive promotion and school selection
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsSection Chief, FDC NCOIC
SchoolsBLC, ALC, Master Gunner
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER performance

The NCO corps is the backbone of Field Artillery. Sergeants (E-5) serve as section chiefs or FDC NCOs, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) lead at the platoon and battery level. This is where artillery leaders are forged — directly responsible for crew training, gunnery qualification, and fire mission execution.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. The FA Master Gunner course is the premier technical qualification for artillery NCOs and significantly improves promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Section Chief — leads a howitzer section through all gunnery tables
  • SSG (E-6): Platoon Sergeant or Chief of Firing Battery — responsible for all section training
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • FA Master Gunner course is the gold standard for technical expertise
  • Pursue JFO certification, Ranger School, or drill sergeant duty
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsSmoke, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior NCOs represent the most experienced and capable leaders in Field Artillery. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as "Smoke" (Chief of Firing Battery) or Operations NCOs, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at battery level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and higher.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of successful gunnery qualifications, advanced school completion (especially FA Master Gunner), and consistent top-block evaluations are essential.

  • SFC (E-7): Chief of Firing Battery ("Smoke") — senior tactical NCO for the battery
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — battery-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Instructor and drill sergeant duty build competitive records
OCS
Officer Commissioning
PathOCS / Green-to-Gold
Duration12-16 Weeks (OCS)
LocationFort Moore, GA
Result2LT Commission
BonusBranch selection

Enlisted Field Artillery Soldiers with leadership potential and a bachelor's degree (or willingness to earn one) can pursue commissioning through Officer Candidate School (OCS) or the Green-to-Gold program. OCS is a 12-week intensive course at Fort Moore, while Green-to-Gold sends soldiers to college full-time with an ROTC scholarship.

Former enlisted artillerymen who commission as FA Officers bring invaluable tactical experience. Many of the Army's best fire support officers and battery commanders came up through the enlisted ranks, with deep knowledge of gunnery and fire direction operations.

  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — rapid commissioning path
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • Field Artillery Basic Officer Leader Course (FABOLC) follows commissioning
  • Ranger School highly encouraged for FA Officers
  • Prior enlisted experience is a massive advantage in battery leadership
  • Career progression: FDO → XO → BTY CDR → BN/BDE Staff → BN CDR

Common Questions
Training
Want to Call for Fire?

13F Fire Support Specialists operate on the front lines with maneuver units, calling for and adjusting indirect fire. Master the call-for-fire format, observer-target factors, and digital fire control to become an elite FIST member.

Gunnery
Struggling with Gunnery?

Gunnery tables are the measure of a cannon section. From dry-fire drills to live-fire qualification, master your crew duties, safety procedures, and fire commands. Repetition builds speed and accuracy under pressure.

Technical
Need AFATDS Skills?

AFATDS is the nerve center of fire direction. 13Js and FDC personnel must master mission processing, system configuration, and network operations. Hands-on time with the system is the fastest path to proficiency.

Schools
Eyeing JFO Certification?

Joint Fires Observer certification qualifies you to call for and control joint fires including CAS and naval gunfire. It's one of the most respected qualifications in the fires community and opens doors to joint assignments.

Career
How Do I Become a FIST?

Fire Support Teams (FISTs) are embedded with maneuver companies. Enlist as a 13F or volunteer for FIST duty as an NCO. Strong land navigation, communication skills, and infantry tactics knowledge are essential.

Promotion
How Fast Can I Promote?

Field Artillery promotion to E-5 averages 3-4 years. BLC completion, a strong NCOER, Master Gunner qualification, and additional schools like Airborne or Ranger dramatically accelerate the timeline.
