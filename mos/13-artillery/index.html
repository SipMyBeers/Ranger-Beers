What is Field Artillery?

Field Artillery has been a decisive arm of the United States Army since the Revolutionary War. Known as the "King of Battle," the FA branch provides fire support to maneuver forces by delivering lethal and non-lethal fires at extended ranges. From towed howitzers and self-propelled cannons to the M270 MLRS and M142 HIMARS rocket systems, Field Artillery delivers precision strikes that shape the battlefield before ground forces ever make contact with the enemy.

Modern Field Artillery has evolved far beyond traditional cannon fire. The branch now encompasses fire support specialists who coordinate joint fires from the front lines, radar operators who detect incoming threats and locate enemy firing positions, and fire control specialists who compute firing data with digital systems. The FA community played a pivotal role in recent conflicts, with HIMARS becoming one of the most strategically significant weapons systems in modern warfare.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Redleg

  • Step 1 Basic Combat Training — Complete 10 weeks of BCT to learn fundamental Soldier skills including marksmanship, physical fitness, drill and ceremony, and basic field craft.
  • Step 2 9-Week AIT at Ft Sill, OK — Advanced Individual Training at the Fires Center of Excellence. Learn your specific MOS — whether cannon operations, fire support procedures, radar systems, or rocket/missile systems.
  • Step 3 First Unit Assignment — Report to your first duty station and integrate into a Field Artillery battery or fire support element. Build proficiency through live-fire exercises and field training.
  • Step 4 FDC Certification — Achieve Fire Direction Center certification and demonstrate mastery of your platform — whether howitzer, HIMARS, or fire support systems.
  • Step 5 NCOES & FA Master Gunner — Progress through BLC, ALC, and SLC. Senior NCOs pursue the prestigious FA Master Gunner course to become the technical expert in their organization.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Field Artillery Soldiers can pursue a range of advanced training opportunities. Available courses include: FA Master Gunner Course, Fire Support Certification, Radar Leader Course, Joint Fires Observer Course, and Ranger School.
