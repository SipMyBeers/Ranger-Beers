Career Field at a Glance

14-43 Week AIT — 10+ MOS — 1 Mission: Communications

The 25-Series is the Army's premier communications and information technology force. From satellite terminals to tactical radios, Signal Soldiers ensure that information flows across every battlefield and garrison network.

MOS Specialties
25B
Information Technology Specialist
AIT20 Weeks
LocationFort Eisenhower, GA
ASVABST: 95
ClearanceSecret
Key SkillsNetwork Admin, Hardware/Software Troubleshooting, Help Desk, Server Mgmt

The 25B Information Technology Specialist is the backbone of the Army's IT infrastructure. 25Bs install, configure, and maintain the networks, servers, and workstations that enable every unit to operate. AIT at Fort Eisenhower is a 20-week course that builds proficiency in networking fundamentals, system administration, and cybersecurity basics.

25Bs earn the CompTIA Security+ certification during AIT — a credential that translates directly to civilian IT careers. They serve in every type of unit from infantry battalions to theater-level signal commands.

  • Network administration and TCP/IP configuration
  • Hardware and software troubleshooting across Windows and Linux
  • Help desk operations and trouble ticket management
  • Server management, Active Directory, and Group Policy
  • CompTIA Security+ certification (earned during AIT)
  • Network security fundamentals and vulnerability scanning
  • Field-expedient networking and tactical infrastructure setup
25S
Satellite Communication Systems Operator-Maintainer
AIT20-24 Weeks
LocationFort Eisenhower, GA
ASVABEL: 98
ClearanceSecret / TS
Key SkillsSATCOM Terminals, Troposcatter, Satellite Network Mgmt, VSAT

The 25S Satellite Communication Systems Operator-Maintainer is responsible for the Army's beyond-line-of-sight communication capability. 25Ss install, operate, and maintain satellite communication terminals and troposcatter systems that provide long-haul connectivity across theaters of operation.

25Ss work with some of the most advanced communication equipment in the military, from VSAT terminals to wideband SATCOM systems. This MOS requires strong technical aptitude and often leads to positions supporting strategic-level communications.

  • SATCOM terminal installation, operation, and maintenance
  • Troposcatter system setup and signal optimization
  • Satellite network management and link budgets
  • VSAT operations and bandwidth allocation
  • Antenna alignment and RF signal theory
  • Multiplexing equipment and digital transmission systems
  • Tactical satellite planning for deployed operations
25U
Signal Support Systems Specialist
AIT14-16 Weeks
LocationFort Eisenhower, GA
ASVABEL: 93
ClearanceSecret
Key SkillsTactical Radios, SINCGARS, JCR/BFT, Network Ops, Retransmission

The 25U Signal Support Systems Specialist is the most common Signal MOS, found in virtually every company-level unit in the Army. 25Us install, operate, and maintain tactical communications equipment including radios, network systems, and battlefield tracking devices that keep units connected during operations.

25Us are the first line of Signal support at the tactical level. They maintain SINCGARS radios, configure JCR/BFT terminals, establish retransmission sites, and troubleshoot communications issues in the field — often under austere conditions.

  • SINCGARS radio programming, operation, and troubleshooting
  • JCR/BFT (Joint Capabilities Release / Blue Force Tracker) operations
  • Tactical network setup and configuration
  • Retransmission site selection, setup, and monitoring
  • Signal operating instructions (SOI) and COMSEC management
  • Antenna theory and field-expedient antenna construction
  • Vehicle-mounted communications system installation and maintenance
25Z
Senior Signal Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE Level
DutyS-6 NCOIC
Key SkillsSignal Ops, Network Architecture, BN/BDE S-6 NCOIC

The 25Z Senior Signal Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted Signal leadership. 25Zs serve as operations sergeants major, S-6 NCOICs, and command sergeants major at battalion and brigade level. They are responsible for planning, implementing, and maintaining the unit's entire communications architecture.

Reaching 25Z requires decades of Signal experience across multiple MOS codes, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a proven record of technical excellence and leadership across tactical and strategic assignments.

  • Signal operations planning and network architecture design
  • BN/BDE S-6 NCOIC duties and staff coordination
  • Communications security (COMSEC) program management
  • Network infrastructure oversight and capacity planning
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Mentorship of junior Signal NCOs across all 25-series MOS
  • Integration of Signal capabilities into maneuver operations

Junior enlisted Signal Soldiers begin their career operating and maintaining communications equipment at the company or battalion level. Depending on MOS, they may work a help desk, operate tactical radios, or maintain satellite terminals. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years.

This is the time to earn industry certifications (Security+, Network+, CCNA), build technical depth, and establish the foundation for NCO leadership. Signal MOS qualifies soldiers for some of the Army's most marketable technical training.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Apprentice operator, supervised tasks
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient operator, independent troubleshooting
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Experienced technician, mentor to new arrivals
  • Earn CompTIA Security+, Network+, or A+ certifications
  • Pursue Cisco CCNA or Microsoft certifications through Army funding
  • Volunteer for deployments and field exercises to build operational experience
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsSection Chief, Shift NCOIC
SchoolsBLC, ALC, CCNA/CCNP
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER + Certifications

Signal NCOs lead teams of 3-8 Soldiers responsible for operating and maintaining communication systems. Sergeants (E-5) serve as section chiefs or shift NCOICs in network operations centers, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) lead larger sections and manage network segments.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Advanced technical certifications and NCOES completion significantly improve promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Section Chief — leads 3-5 soldiers, manages equipment accountability
  • SSG (E-6): Shift NCOIC / Section NCOIC — manages network operations
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • Pursue CCNA, CCNP, or CISSP for competitive advantage
  • Joint Network Operations Course and advanced vendor training
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsS-6 NCOIC, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior Signal NCOs represent the most experienced and capable technical leaders in the branch. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as S-6 NCOICs or platoon sergeants in Signal companies, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level, and Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and higher as the 25Z Senior Signal Sergeant.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of technical excellence, advanced certifications, NCOES completion, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential.

  • SFC (E-7): S-6 NCOIC or Signal Platoon Sergeant — manages section of 15-30 soldiers
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — company-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): 25Z Senior Signal Sergeant — BN/BDE signal operations
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Network architecture and strategic communications planning
WO
Warrant Officer / Officer Track
PathWOCS / OCS / Green-to-Gold
Duration5-7 Weeks (WOCS)
LocationFort Novosel / Fort Eisenhower
ResultWO1 / 2LT Commission
Specialty255A / 255N / 25A

Experienced Signal NCOs with deep technical expertise can pursue the Warrant Officer track (255-series) or commission as Signal Officers (25A). Warrant Officers serve as the Army's premier Signal technicians — subject matter experts in network engineering, satellite systems, and information services management.

Signal Warrant Officers (255A, 255N) are in high demand across the Army. The OCS or Green-to-Gold path leads to Signal Officer (25A) positions, where prior enlisted experience in network operations provides an enormous advantage in understanding the technical challenges at the tactical level.

  • WOCS: 5-7 weeks at Fort Novosel — Warrant Officer Candidate School
  • 255A: Information Services Technician — network engineering SME
  • 255N: Network Management Technician — enterprise network operations
  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — commission as 25A Signal Officer
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • Prior enlisted Signal experience is a massive advantage in technical leadership

Common Questions
Certifications
Need CompTIA Certs?

The Army funds Security+, Network+, and A+ exams for Signal Soldiers. Many 25Bs earn Security+ during AIT. These certifications are required for DoD 8570 compliance and translate directly to civilian IT careers.

Training
Struggling with Networking?

TCP/IP, subnetting, and routing fundamentals are the backbone of Signal training. Master the OSI model and practice with Packet Tracer before you arrive at Fort Eisenhower. It will pay dividends in AIT.

Career
Want SATCOM Skills?

25S SATCOM operators work with cutting-edge satellite terminals and troposcatter systems. High ASVAB scores and a TS clearance open the door to strategic-level communications assignments.

Schools
How Long is 25B AIT?

25B AIT at Fort Eisenhower is 20 weeks. The course covers networking, system administration, and cybersecurity fundamentals. You'll earn CompTIA Security+ before graduation.

MOS
Radio vs IT MOS?

25U is tactical radios and field communications — you'll be in the field setting up retrans and programming SINCGARS. 25B is networks and servers — you'll manage infrastructure and troubleshoot systems. Both are in high demand.

Transition
Civilian Tech Career?

Signal skills translate directly to civilian IT and telecom careers. Network admin, systems engineer, SATCOM tech, and cybersecurity analyst roles all map to 25-series experience. The certifications you earn in the Army are industry-standard.
