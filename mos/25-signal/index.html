Career Field Overview

What is Signal?

The Signal branch provides the communications backbone for the entire Army. Signal Soldiers install, operate, and maintain the tactical and strategic communication systems that enable commanders to exercise command and control across the battlefield. From satellite terminals to fiber optic networks to tactical radios, Signal ensures that information flows from the foxhole to the Pentagon.

Training is centered at Ft Eisenhower, Georgia, the Army's Cyber Center of Excellence. The 25-series is one of the most diverse career fields, with over 10 MOS codes covering everything from IT help desk operations to satellite communications to network defense. Signal skills translate directly to civilian IT and telecommunications careers, and many Signal Soldiers earn industry certifications like CompTIA Network+, Security+, and CCNA during their service.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Signal Specialist

  • Step 1BCT — Complete Basic Combat Training before entering Signal AIT at Ft Eisenhower.
  • Step 214-20 Week AIT at Ft Eisenhower, GA — MOS-specific training in networking, radio systems, satellite communications, or IT operations. Includes hands-on lab work and field exercises.
  • Step 3Unit Assignment — Report to a Signal battalion, Brigade Signal company, or staff section. Begin operating and maintaining unit communication systems.
  • Step 4Industry Certifications — Earn CompTIA Network+, Security+, CCNA, or other certifications. The Army funds certification exams for Signal Soldiers.
  • Step 5NCOES & Advanced Courses — Progress through NCO Education System. Pursue Joint Network Operations, advanced Cisco training, or transition to Cyber (17-series) MOS.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Signal Soldiers can pursue: CompTIA Network+, Security+, A+, Cisco CCNA/CCNP, Joint Network Operations Course, Satellite Controller Certification, and various vendor-specific training. Senior Signal NCOs may attend the Signal Senior Leader Course or transition to the Cyber branch.
