Career Field at a Glance

22-Week OSUT — 3 MOS — 1 Mission: Armored Warfare

The 19-Series is the Army's armored combat force. From M1 Abrams tankers to Cavalry Scouts on the forward edge, Armor Soldiers deliver overwhelming firepower and shock action in every fight.

MOS Specialties
19K
M1 Armor Crewman
OSUT22 Weeks
LocationFort Moore, GA
ASVABCO: 87
DutyCombat Arms
Key SkillsGunnery, Tank Ops, Maintenance, Crew Drills

The 19K M1 Armor Crewman operates the M1 Abrams — one of the most lethal main battle tanks in the world. Tankers crew a 70-ton, 1,500-horsepower platform armed with a 120mm smoothbore cannon, delivering devastating firepower and shock action on the modern battlefield. OSUT at Fort Moore combines Basic Combat Training and Advanced Individual Training into a continuous 22-week course.

Training progresses from individual soldier skills through crew-level gunnery tables, culminating in live-fire maneuver exercises. Graduates are qualified in M1 Abrams operations, gunnery, maintenance, and combined arms maneuver.

  • M1 Abrams tank operations: driving, loading, gunning, and tank commanding
  • Gunnery tables I through XII — individual through platoon live fire
  • Preventive maintenance checks and services (PMCS) on the M1 platform
  • Crew drills: react to contact, action on FASCAM, breach obstacle
  • Combined arms maneuver with infantry and artillery
  • Mounted and dismounted land navigation
  • Field training exercise (FTX) with live-fire gunnery lanes
19D
Cavalry Scout
OSUT22 Weeks
LocationFort Moore, GA
ASVABCO: 87
DutyCombat Arms
Key SkillsRecon, Surveillance, Bradley Ops, Target Acquisition

The 19D Cavalry Scout is the eyes and ears of the battlefield. Cav Scouts conduct reconnaissance, surveillance, and security operations far forward of friendly lines, providing commanders with critical intelligence on enemy disposition, terrain, and obstacles. They operate Bradley Fighting Vehicles, HMMWVs, and Strykers depending on the unit type.

19Ds complete 22-week OSUT at Fort Moore with specialized training in reconnaissance techniques, reporting procedures, and mounted/dismounted operations. Scouts must master route, zone, and area reconnaissance as well as screening operations.

  • Route, zone, and area reconnaissance operations
  • Surveillance and target acquisition techniques
  • Bradley Fighting Vehicle operations and gunnery
  • Screening operations and counter-reconnaissance
  • Call for fire and close air support procedures
  • Mounted and dismounted patrol operations
  • SALUTE and intelligence reporting, map overlay production
19Z
Senior Armor Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE Level
DutySenior Enlisted Leader
Key SkillsMaster Gunner, Training Mgmt, Armor Operations

The 19Z Senior Armor Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted Armor leadership. 19Zs serve as operations sergeants major, first sergeants, and command sergeants major at battalion and brigade level. They are responsible for BN/BDE armor operations, training management, and the tactical employment of the unit's armored combat power.

Reaching 19Z requires decades of Armor experience, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, the Master Gunner track, and a record of proven performance across multiple duty positions in both tank and cavalry organizations.

  • BN/BDE armor operations planning and execution
  • Master Gunner track — unit gunnery training expert
  • Training management and readiness reporting at BN/BDE level
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Doctrine development and combined arms integration
  • Mentorship of junior NCOs and officer integration
  • Combat leadership at the highest enlisted levels

E1-E4
Junior Enlisted

Junior enlisted Armor Soldiers begin their career as loaders and drivers on the M1 Abrams or as scouts in a cavalry troop. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years and brings increased responsibility as a gunner or vehicle commander trainee.

This is the time to master your platform, qualify on gunnery tables, and build the foundation for NCO leadership. Pursue additional schools and volunteer for deployment opportunities.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Loader/Driver, basic crew tasks
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient crew member, gunnery qualification
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Gunner position, mentor to new arrivals
  • Pursue Airborne, Air Assault, or Ranger School early
  • Volunteer for deployment and gunnery competitions
  • Build PT score for competitive promotion and school selection
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsTC, Section Sgt, PSG
SchoolsBLC, ALC, Master Gunner
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER performance

The NCO corps is the backbone of Armor. Sergeants (E-5) serve as tank commanders or scout section leaders, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) lead sections and platoons. This is where Armor leaders are forged — directly responsible for crew training, vehicle maintenance, and tactical employment in combined arms operations.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Master Gunner School and Ranger School significantly improve promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Tank Commander / Scout Section Leader — owns the crew and vehicle
  • SSG (E-6): Section Sergeant — leads 2-4 vehicles, manages gunnery training
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • Master Gunner Course is the premier technical school for Armor NCOs
  • Pursue Ranger, Airborne, RSLC, or Cavalry Leader Course
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsPSG, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior NCOs represent the most experienced and capable leaders in Armor. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as Platoon Sergeants, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company/troop level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and higher.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of combat leadership, Master Gunner qualification, advanced school completion, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential.

  • SFC (E-7): Platoon Sergeant — senior tactical NCO for the platoon's vehicles and crews
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — company/troop-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Master Gunner and instructor duty build competitive records
OCS
Officer Commissioning
PathOCS / Green-to-Gold
Duration12-16 Weeks (OCS)
LocationFort Moore, GA
Result2LT Commission
BonusBranch selection

Enlisted Armor Soldiers with leadership potential and a bachelor's degree (or willingness to earn one) can pursue commissioning through Officer Candidate School (OCS) or the Green-to-Gold program. OCS is a 12-week intensive course at Fort Moore, while Green-to-Gold sends soldiers to college full-time with an ROTC scholarship.

Former enlisted Armor soldiers who commission as Armor Officers bring invaluable tactical and gunnery experience that sets them apart from their peers. Understanding crew dynamics and vehicle operations from the ground up makes for exceptional platoon leaders.

  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — rapid commissioning path
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • Armor Basic Officer Leader Course (ABOLC) follows commissioning
  • Ranger School and Maneuver Captain's Career Course for career progression
  • Prior enlisted experience is a massive advantage in platoon leadership
  • Career progression: PL → XO → CO CDR → BN/BDE Staff → BN CDR

Common Questions
Career
Want to Drive a Tank?

19K tankers crew the M1 Abrams — 70 tons of American firepower. OSUT at Fort Moore teaches you to drive, load, gun, and command the most lethal main battle tank on the planet.

MOS
Cav Scout or Tanker?

19D Scouts are the eyes and ears of the battlefield. 19K Tankers bring the thunder. Both share the same Armor branch and OSUT location, but the mission and platform are different. Choose your path.

Training
Struggling with Gunnery Tables?

Gunnery qualification is the defining skill in Armor. Master your fire commands, engagement techniques, and target identification. Table VIII crew qualification is what separates good crews from great ones.

Schools
Need Master Gunner?

The Master Gunner Course is the premier technical school for Armor NCOs. Master Gunners are the unit's gunnery experts, advising commanders on weapons employment and managing gunnery programs.

Platform
Bradley or Abrams?

19Ks crew the M1 Abrams tank. 19Ds typically operate Bradleys, HMMWVs, or Strykers depending on the unit. Both platforms are lethal — your MOS determines which you'll master first.

Promotion
How Fast Can I Promote?

Armor promotion to E-5 averages 3-4 years. BLC completion, a strong NCOER, Master Gunner qualification, and Ranger tab dramatically accelerate the timeline.
