Career Field Overview

What is Armor?

The Armor Branch is the Army's mounted combat arm, employing tanks, Bradley Fighting Vehicles, Strykers, and other armored platforms to deliver overwhelming firepower and shock action. Cavalry Scouts serve as the eyes and ears of the battlefield, conducting reconnaissance and security operations far forward of friendly lines. Tankers crew the M1 Abrams, one of the most lethal main battle tanks in the world.

Armor Soldiers train at Ft Moore, Georgia — the Home of Maneuver — alongside Infantry. The Armor branch traces its lineage to the horse cavalry and has evolved into a highly mobile, lethal force capable of decisive action in any environment. From the deserts of Iraq to the training grounds of Europe, Armor units are a cornerstone of combined arms warfare.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Crewman

  • Step 1 22-Week OSUT at Ft Moore, GA — One Station Unit Training covers basic soldiering skills plus platform-specific training on the M1 Abrams or Bradley Fighting Vehicle. Includes gunnery, mounted maneuver, and reconnaissance fundamentals.
  • Step 2 First Unit Assignment — Report to an Armored Brigade Combat Team or Cavalry squadron. Begin crew qualification, participate in gunnery tables, and learn unit-level tactics.
  • Step 3 Master Gunner Course — Highly competitive course that produces unit gunnery experts. Master Gunners advise commanders on weapons employment and manage gunnery training programs.
  • Step 4 NCOES (BLC / ALC / SLC) — NCO Education System courses develop leadership and technical expertise at each rank. Armor-specific modules cover platoon and company-level tactics.
  • Step 5 Advanced Schools — Senior Armor NCOs pursue Ranger School, RSLC, Sniper School, Airborne, or Maneuver Senior Leader Course to broaden their expertise.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Armor Soldiers have access to advanced training including: Master Gunner Course, Reconnaissance and Surveillance Leaders Course (RSLC), Sniper School, Ranger School, Airborne School, Bradley Leader Course, Stryker Leader Course, and the Cavalry Leader Course.
