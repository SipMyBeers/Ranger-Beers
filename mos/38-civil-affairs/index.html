What is Civil Affairs?

Civil Affairs (CA) is a special operations branch that serves as the bridge between military forces and civilian populations, governments, and organizations. CA Soldiers assess civil conditions in operational areas, advise commanders on the impact of military operations on civilians, and coordinate humanitarian assistance, governance support, and infrastructure restoration.

Active-duty Civil Affairs forces are stationed at Ft Liberty, North Carolina, and are part of U.S. Army Special Operations Command. Army Reserve CA units are spread across the country. CA Soldiers deploy to some of the most complex environments in the world, working in unstable regions where the line between combat and humanitarian operations blurs. Cultural awareness, negotiation skills, and the ability to operate independently are essential.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to CA Specialist

  • Step 1BCT / OCS — Complete Basic Combat Training or Officer Candidate School before entering the Civil Affairs pipeline.
  • Step 2CA Qualification Course at Ft Liberty — The CAQC teaches civil reconnaissance, governance assessment, humanitarian operations, and tactical CA team operations. Includes assessment and selection.
  • Step 3Airborne School — Active-duty CA Soldiers attend Airborne School at Ft Moore, GA, for assignment to airborne-capable CA units.
  • Step 4Language Training — Many CA Soldiers attend the Defense Language Institute or in-country immersion programs to develop language skills essential for their mission.
  • Step 5Unit Assignment & Advanced Courses — Serve in CA teams at Ft Liberty or deployed. Pursue governance, development studies, and NCOES development.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Civil Affairs Soldiers can pursue: Defense Language Institute (DLI), Governance & Development courses, Humanitarian Assistance programs, Airborne School, Cultural Studies, interagency coordination courses, and joint civil-military operations training.
