Career Field at a Glance

7-12 Week AIT — 5+ MOS — 1 Mission: Movement & Distribution

The 88-Series is the Army's lifeline. From convoy operations across hostile terrain to watercraft missions on open seas, Transportation Soldiers ensure nothing stops moving.

MOS Specialties
88M
Motor Transport Operator
AIT7 Weeks
LocationFort Gregg-Adams, VA
CertificationCDL Qualification
DutyCombat Support
Key SkillsConvoy Ops, Vehicle Maint, Load Planning, Hazmat

The 88M Motor Transport Operator is the backbone of the Transportation Corps. 88Ms drive everything from HMMWVs and LMTVs to Heavy Equipment Transporters (HETs), moving soldiers, ammunition, fuel, and equipment across every type of terrain in any conditions. AIT at Fort Gregg-Adams, Virginia, transforms soldiers into qualified military truck drivers with a CDL that transfers directly to civilian careers.

Training covers vehicle inspection, preventive maintenance, convoy operations, defensive driving, load planning, and hazmat transport. Graduates are immediately deployable and will spend their career behind the wheel, keeping the Army's supply lines open.

  • CDL qualification on military tactical vehicles
  • Convoy operations and route planning in hostile environments
  • Preventive maintenance checks and services (PMCS)
  • Load planning, weight distribution, and cargo securement
  • Hazmat transport procedures and safety compliance
  • Defensive driving and rollover avoidance techniques
  • Night and blackout driving operations
88N
Transportation Management Coordinator
AIT10-12 Weeks
LocationFort Gregg-Adams, VA
FocusMovement Control
DutyCombat Support
Key SkillsCargo Tracking, Logistics, Port Ops, ITV Systems

The 88N Transportation Management Coordinator is the logistician behind the movement. 88Ns plan, coordinate, and track the flow of cargo, personnel, and equipment through the Army's distribution network. They operate in movement control teams, ports, railheads, and logistics operations centers, ensuring that the right supplies reach the right place at the right time.

Training covers the Army's In-Transit Visibility (ITV) systems, cargo documentation, movement planning, port operations, and intermodal coordination. 88Ns are essential to sustainment operations and develop skills that translate directly to civilian supply chain and logistics management careers.

  • Movement control operations and transportation planning
  • Cargo tracking using In-Transit Visibility (ITV) systems
  • Logistics planning and distribution management
  • Port and terminal operations coordination
  • Intermodal transportation (road, rail, air, sea)
  • Transportation documentation and regulatory compliance
  • Automated movement and logistics information systems
88K
Watercraft Operator
AIT12 Weeks
LocationFort Eustis, VA
FocusVessel Operations
DutyCombat Support
Key SkillsHarbor Craft, Inshore/Offshore, Marine Engineering

The 88K Watercraft Operator is one of the Army's most unique MOS specialties. 88Ks operate Army vessels ranging from harbor tugs and landing craft to logistics support vessels (LSVs), conducting inshore and offshore operations that move troops, vehicles, and cargo where roads and airfields don't exist. This is the Army's Navy.

Training covers vessel navigation, marine engineering fundamentals, harbor operations, deck seamanship, and watercraft safety. 88Ks serve at Army watercraft units stationed at coastal installations and deploy worldwide to support joint logistics-over-the-shore (JLOTS) operations.

  • Army vessel operations — tugs, landing craft, LSVs
  • Harbor craft navigation and piloting
  • Inshore and offshore watercraft operations
  • Marine engineering and vessel maintenance
  • Deck seamanship, line handling, and anchoring
  • JLOTS (Joint Logistics Over-the-Shore) operations
  • Maritime safety, damage control, and emergency procedures
88Z
Senior Transportation Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE Level
DutySenior Enlisted Leader
Key SkillsMovement Planning, Trans Ops, Doctrine, Mentorship

The 88Z Senior Transportation Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted Transportation leadership. 88Zs serve as operations sergeants major, first sergeants, and command sergeants major at battalion and brigade level. They oversee all transportation operations, movement planning, and distribution management across their organization.

Reaching 88Z requires decades of transportation experience across multiple MOS specialties, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a proven record of performance in increasingly complex logistics assignments.

  • BN/BDE transportation operations management
  • Movement planning and distribution oversight
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Doctrine development and transportation innovation
  • Unit readiness and logistics resource management
  • Mentorship of junior NCOs and officer integration
  • Senior enlisted leadership at the highest levels

Junior Enlisted

CertsCDL, Hazmat
Pay$24K-$36K/yr
BonusUp to $20K (initial)

Junior enlisted Transportation Soldiers begin their career as vehicle operators, cargo handlers, or watercraft crew members. This is where you earn your CDL, learn convoy procedures, and build the hands-on experience that defines the Transportation Corps. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years.

Use this time to earn additional vehicle licenses, hazmat endorsements, and Master Driver qualification. Build a strong foundation of technical competence and leadership potential for NCO development.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Vehicle operator, basic soldier tasks
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Qualified driver, additional vehicle certifications
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Experienced operator, convoy team member
  • Earn CDL and pursue hazmat endorsements early
  • Volunteer for deployment and convoy missions
  • Build PT score and pursue Master Driver qualification
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsTC, Convoy CDR, Dispatcher
SchoolsBLC, ALC, Master Driver
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER performance

Transportation NCOs lead crews, convoys, and movement control teams. Sergeants (E-5) serve as truck commanders and team leaders, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) lead sections and serve as convoy commanders responsible for the safe delivery of critical supplies through hostile territory.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Master Driver Trainer certification and additional vehicle qualifications significantly improve promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Truck Commander / Team Leader — leads vehicle crews
  • SSG (E-6): Section Sergeant / Convoy Commander — leads convoy operations
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • Master Driver Trainer Course enhances career progression
  • Pursue Convoy Live Fire and Movement Control courses
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsPSG, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior NCOs represent the most experienced leaders in the Transportation Corps. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as Platoon Sergeants overseeing convoy platoons and movement control sections. Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and higher.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of successful movement operations, advanced course completion, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential for selection.

  • SFC (E-7): Platoon Sergeant — senior tactical NCO for transportation platoon
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — company-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Instructor and drill sergeant duty build competitive records
TC
Transportation Corps Officer
PathOCS / Green-to-Gold
Duration12-16 Weeks (OCS)
LocationFort Moore, GA
Result2LT Commission
BranchTransportation Corps

Enlisted Transportation Soldiers with leadership potential and a bachelor's degree (or willingness to earn one) can pursue commissioning through Officer Candidate School (OCS) or the Green-to-Gold program. Upon commissioning, officers attend the Transportation Basic Officer Leader Course (TBOLC) at Fort Gregg-Adams before leading transportation platoons.

Former enlisted soldiers who commission as Transportation Corps Officers bring invaluable hands-on logistics experience. Many of the Army's best sustainment leaders came up through the enlisted ranks, understanding distribution operations from the driver's seat to the operations center.

  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — rapid commissioning path
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • Transportation Basic Officer Leader Course (TBOLC) follows commissioning
  • TC officers manage movement operations at BN/BDE level
  • Prior enlisted experience is a major advantage in logistics leadership
  • Career progression: PL → XO → CO CDR → BN/BDE Staff → BN CDR

Common Questions
Career
Want a CDL?

88M Soldiers earn a military CDL during AIT that transfers directly to civilian trucking careers. It's one of the most valuable certifications the Army offers — and it's free.

MOS
Love Driving Trucks?

88M Motor Transport Operators drive everything from tactical trucks to Heavy Equipment Transporters across the world. If you want to be behind the wheel, this is your MOS.

Unique
Interested in Watercraft?

The Army has boats. 88K Watercraft Operators run landing craft, tugs, and logistics support vessels. It's one of the most unique jobs in the entire military.

Training
Convoy Operations?

Convoy operations are the bread and butter of the Transportation Corps. Learn route planning, threat mitigation, and how to move critical supplies through hostile territory.

Schools
Logistics Career?

Transportation experience translates directly to civilian logistics, supply chain management, and commercial trucking careers. The skills you learn are in high demand worldwide.

Promotion
How Fast Can I Promote?

Transportation promotion to E-5 averages 3-4 years. BLC completion, strong NCOERs, Master Driver certification, and deployment experience accelerate the timeline.
