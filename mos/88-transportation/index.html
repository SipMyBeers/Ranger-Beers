Career Field Overview

What is Transportation?

The Transportation branch is responsible for moving everything the Army needs to fight — soldiers, ammunition, fuel, food, equipment, and vehicles — by ground, water, rail, and air. Motor Transport Operators (88M) are the backbone of the branch, driving everything from HMMWVs to Heavy Equipment Transporters across hostile terrain. Watercraft operators keep Army vessels running on rivers, harbors, and open seas worldwide.

Training is centered at Ft Gregg-Adams (formerly Ft Lee), Virginia, home of the Army Combined Arms Support Command. The Transportation branch is critical to sustainment operations — without it, combat forces cannot be supplied, reinforced, or repositioned. The 88-series offers a CDL (Commercial Driver's License) and other certifications that translate directly to well-paying civilian transportation and logistics careers.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Transporter

  • Step 1BCT — Complete Basic Combat Training before reporting to Transportation AIT.
  • Step 27-12 Week AIT at Ft Gregg-Adams, VA — MOS-specific training in vehicle operations, cargo handling, watercraft, or transportation management. 88M Soldiers earn a military CDL.
  • Step 3Unit Assignment — Report to a Transportation company or battalion. Begin convoy operations, cargo distribution, or movement control missions.
  • Step 4Master Driver & Certifications — Pursue Master Driver Course to become the unit's driver training expert. Earn hazmat endorsements and additional vehicle licenses.
  • Step 5NCOES & Advanced Logistics — Progress through NCOES while pursuing Convoy Live Fire, Movement Control, and Multimodal Operations courses.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Transportation Soldiers can pursue: Master Driver Trainer Course, Convoy Live Fire Exercise, Movement Control Course, Multimodal Operations, Hazmat certification, and various CDL endorsements. Senior NCOs attend the Transportation Senior Leader Course and may serve as movement control advisors.
