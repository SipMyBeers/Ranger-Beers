Career Field Overview

Guardians Against WMD

CBRN specialists are the Army's first line of defense against chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats. The 74D MOS is responsible for detecting, identifying, and mitigating CBRN hazards in both combat and garrison environments. These soldiers operate specialized detection equipment, conduct decontamination operations, and advise commanders on protective measures to keep the force safe from weapons of mass destruction.

In an era of evolving threats, CBRN specialists play a critical role in homeland defense, consequence management, and weapons of mass destruction civil support teams (WMD-CST). They train alongside first responders, conduct hazmat operations, and maintain readiness for scenarios ranging from industrial accidents to deliberate CBRN attacks. The career field offers unique expertise that is highly valued in both military and civilian sectors, including emergency management and hazardous materials response.

Training Pipeline

Becoming a CBRN Specialist

  • Phase 1 Basic Combat Training — 10 weeks of BCT covering warrior tasks, physical fitness, and fundamental soldiering skills.
  • Phase 2 12-Week AIT — Advanced Individual Training at Ft Leonard Wood, MO. Learn CBRN detection equipment operation, decontamination procedures, hazard prediction, and protective measures.
  • Phase 3 Unit Assignment — Report to your first unit as the CBRN specialist. Serve as the subject matter expert on chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear defense for your commander.
  • Phase 4 Hazmat Certification — Obtain hazardous materials certifications and additional technical qualifications for detection and decontamination systems.
  • Phase 5 NCOES — Noncommissioned Officer Education System courses for leadership development and career progression within the CBRN field.
  • Phase 6 Advanced Assignments — Opportunities for WMD-CST, technical escort, CBRN reconnaissance, and joint CBRN billets.
Advanced Training

Specialized Courses

CBRN specialists can pursue advanced training in Hazmat Technician certification, WMD Civil Support Team (WMD-CST) qualification, CBRN Reconnaissance operations, Smoke Operations for battlefield obscuration, and Technical Escort for the safe transport and disposal of hazardous materials and munitions.
