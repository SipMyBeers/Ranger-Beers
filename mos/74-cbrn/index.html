Career Field at a Glance

13-Week AIT — 2 MOS — 1 Mission: CBRN Defense

The 74-Series is the Army's specialized force for detecting, mitigating, and defeating chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats. From hazard reconnaissance to full-scale decontamination, CBRN Soldiers protect the force so it can fight.

MOS Specialties
74D
CBRN Specialist
AIT13 Weeks
LocationFort Leonard Wood, MO
ASVABST: 100
DutyCombat Support
Key SkillsRecon, Decon, Hazard Detection, MOPP, Smoke Ops

The 74D CBRN Specialist is the Army's expert on chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear defense. Every brigade and battalion depends on 74Ds to detect, identify, and mitigate CBRN hazards in both combat and garrison environments. The 13-week AIT at Fort Leonard Wood trains soldiers on the full spectrum of CBRN operations, from reconnaissance and detection to large-scale decontamination.

Training progresses from individual equipment proficiency through team and section-level operations, culminating in field training exercises that simulate realistic CBRN scenarios. Graduates are qualified on detection systems, decontamination apparatus, MOPP procedures, and smoke generation equipment.

  • CBRN reconnaissance and hazard area survey operations
  • Decontamination operations (hasty, deliberate, and thorough)
  • Chemical and radiological detection equipment operation (M4 JCAD, AN/PDR-77, ACADA)
  • NBC defense planning and vulnerability assessments
  • Smoke operations for battlefield obscuration (M56 Coyote, M157/M58)
  • MOPP gear procedures and collective protection systems
  • Hazard prediction and contamination plotting (NBC-1 through NBC-6 reports)
74Z
Senior CBRN Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE/DIV Level
DutySenior Enlisted Leader
Key SkillsCBRN Ops Planning, WMD Response, Unit Programs

The 74Z Senior CBRN Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted CBRN leadership. 74Zs serve as CBRN operations sergeants major and senior CBRN NCOs at battalion, brigade, and division level. They are responsible for CBRN operations planning, WMD response coordination, and oversight of unit CBRN defense programs across the formation.

Reaching 74Z requires extensive CBRN experience, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a record of technical excellence and leadership across multiple CBRN duty positions.

  • CBRN operations planning and integration at BN/BDE/DIV level
  • WMD response coordination and consequence management
  • Unit CBRN defense program management and inspections
  • CBRN training management and readiness reporting
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Doctrine development and CBRN tactical innovation
  • Mentorship of junior CBRN NCOs and officer integration
74A
CBRN Officer
CommissionROTC / OCS / USMA
BOLCCBRN BOLC, Ft Leonard Wood
ScopePlatoon to BDE Staff
DutyBranch Officer
Key SkillsCBRN Planning, WMD Policy, Force Protection

The 74A CBRN Officer leads CBRN units and serves as the principal CBRN advisor to commanders at every echelon. CBRN Officers commission through ROTC, OCS, or West Point and attend the CBRN Basic Officer Leader Course (BOLC) at Fort Leonard Wood before taking their first assignment as a CBRN platoon leader.

CBRN Officers progress from platoon leadership through company command and into battalion/brigade staff roles where they plan and synchronize CBRN defense operations. Prior enlisted 74Ds who commission bring invaluable technical expertise to the officer ranks.

  • CBRN BOLC: 19 weeks at Fort Leonard Wood — officer foundation course
  • Platoon Leader: leads CBRN recon or decontamination platoon
  • Company Commander: commands CBRN company (decon, recon, or smoke)
  • BN/BDE CBRN Officer (S-3 CBRN): plans CBRN defense for the formation
  • WMD-CST and CARA assignment opportunities
  • Career progression: PL → XO → CO CDR → BN/BDE Staff → BN CDR

Junior enlisted CBRN Soldiers begin their career as specialists attached to company- and battalion-level units, serving as the unit's subject matter expert on chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear defense. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years and brings increased responsibility for equipment accountability and training.

This is the time to pursue hazmat certifications, attend additional schools, and build technical expertise. CBRN Soldiers often serve as the sole CBRN representative in their unit, requiring independence and initiative from day one.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — CBRN Specialist, learn unit equipment
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient operator, run unit CBRN training
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Unit CBRN expert, mentor to new arrivals
  • Pursue Hazmat Technician and OSHA certifications early
  • Volunteer for WMD-CST and CBRN reconnaissance assignments
  • Build technical proficiency on all detection and decon equipment
E5-E6

KeyNCOER performance

CBRN NCOs lead decontamination teams, reconnaissance sections, and smoke platoon elements. Sergeants (E-5) lead teams of 3-4 Soldiers in CBRN operations, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) serve as section sergeants and CBRN NCOICs at battalion level. This is where CBRN leaders develop the technical depth and leadership skills that define the career field.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Pursuing Hazmat Incident Commander certification and advanced CBRN courses significantly improves promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Team Leader — leads CBRN recon or decon teams
  • SSG (E-6): Section Sergeant / BN CBRN NCOIC — runs unit CBRN program
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • Hazmat Incident Commander and Technical Escort qualifications
  • Pursue instructor duty at the CBRN School, Fort Leonard Wood
  • Strong NCOER evaluations and technical certifications drive promotion

Senior CBRN NCOs represent the most experienced and technically proficient leaders in the field. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as Platoon Sergeants for CBRN platoons, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level or serve as BDE CBRN NCOICs, and Sergeants Major (E-9) serve as the senior CBRN enlisted advisor at division and higher.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of technical excellence, advanced certifications, instructor duty, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential for advancement.

  • SFC (E-7): Platoon Sergeant — leads CBRN recon or decon platoon
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Company First Sergeant or BDE CBRN NCOIC
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Senior CBRN Sergeant Major — division-level advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • WMD-CST leadership and instructor assignments build competitive records

PathCBRN WO / OCS
WOCS7 Weeks
LocationFort Novosel, AL
ResultWO1 Appointment
FocusTechnical CBRN Expert

Experienced CBRN NCOs can pursue the Warrant Officer track to become the Army's premier CBRN technical experts. CBRN Warrant Officers serve as technical advisors to commanders, leading complex CBRN operations and managing advanced detection and analytical capabilities that exceed the scope of enlisted or commissioned officer roles.

The Warrant Officer path allows CBRN professionals to remain technically focused throughout their career without transitioning to general leadership positions. Prior enlisted experience in CBRN reconnaissance, WMD-CST, or technical escort is highly valued.

  • WOCS: 7 weeks at Fort Novosel — Warrant Officer Candidate School
  • CBRN Warrant Officer Basic Course at Fort Leonard Wood
  • Technical advisor to BN/BDE/DIV commanders on CBRN operations
  • Manage advanced CBRN detection and analytical laboratories
  • WMD elimination and exploitation operations
  • Career progression: WO1 → CW2 → CW3 → CW4 → CW5

Common Questions
Career
What is CBRN?

CBRN stands for Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear. 74D Soldiers are the Army's experts on detecting, identifying, and defeating these threats — from nerve agents to dirty bombs to biological weapons.

Training
Afraid of Gas Chamber?

The gas chamber is just the beginning. CBRN Soldiers train extensively in MOPP gear and live-agent environments. You'll build confidence fast — and you'll be the one coaching everyone else through it.

Certifications
Need Hazmat Certs?

CBRN Soldiers earn Hazmat Technician, Hazmat Incident Commander, and OSHA certifications that transfer directly to civilian careers in emergency management and hazardous materials response.

Operations
Smoke Operations?

CBRN Soldiers operate smoke generation systems like the M56 Coyote to provide battlefield obscuration. Smoke ops protect friendly forces during movement, breaching operations, and river crossings.

Mission
Want to Fight WMD?

WMD-Civil Support Teams and CBRN reconnaissance units operate at the cutting edge of threat detection. These elite assignments put CBRN Soldiers on the front line of national defense.

Transition
Civilian HAZMAT Career?

CBRN skills translate directly to civilian HAZMAT, emergency management, EPA, FEMA, and DHS careers. Certifications earned in service give you a head start in a high-demand field.
