Career Field Overview

What is Quartermaster?

The Quartermaster branch is the Army's oldest logistics branch, tracing its origins to 1775. Quartermaster Soldiers manage the supply chain that keeps the Army equipped and sustained — from ordering and receiving supplies to distributing food, fuel, water, ammunition, and spare parts to units in the field. The branch also encompasses field services including mortuary affairs, aerial delivery, and petroleum operations.

Training is centered at Ft Gregg-Adams (formerly Ft Lee), Virginia, home of the Army Combined Arms Support Command and the Quartermaster School. The 92-series offers diverse career paths from automated logistics and supply management to culinary arts and petroleum operations. The skills learned in Quartermaster — supply chain management, logistics, inventory control — are among the most transferable to civilian careers in warehousing, distribution, and supply chain management.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Supply Specialist

  • Step 1BCT — Complete Basic Combat Training before reporting to Quartermaster AIT.
  • Step 28-13 Week AIT at Ft Gregg-Adams, VA — MOS-specific training in supply management, petroleum operations, culinary arts, or mortuary affairs. Includes hands-on work with Army logistics systems like GCSS-Army.
  • Step 3Unit Assignment — Report to a Forward Support Company, Brigade Support Battalion, or Division Sustainment Brigade. Begin managing supplies, operating fuel points, or running field kitchens.
  • Step 4Logistics Systems & Certifications — Earn proficiency in GCSS-Army, Global Combat Support System, and other automated logistics platforms. Pursue ServSafe certification (culinary) or petroleum handling certifications.
  • Step 5NCOES & Advanced Sustainment — Progress through NCOES. Pursue Aerial Delivery & Rigger Course, Petroleum Operations, Mortuary Affairs Advanced Course, or Culinary Arts advanced training.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Quartermaster Soldiers can pursue: Aerial Delivery and Rigger Course (Airborne Rigger), Petroleum Operations Course, Mortuary Affairs Advanced Course, GCSS-Army master trainer, Culinary Arts advanced training, and the Quartermaster Senior Leader Course. 92G Soldiers may attend the Advanced Culinary Arts program and compete in Army culinary competitions.
