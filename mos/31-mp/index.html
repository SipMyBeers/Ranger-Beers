Career Field Overview

Defenders of Order

Military Police soldiers are the Army's law enforcement professionals. From patrolling installations and enforcing traffic laws to conducting high-risk criminal investigations and providing area security in combat zones, the 31-Series covers the full spectrum of police and security operations. MP units deploy worldwide to support maneuver commanders with route security, detainee operations, and law-and-order missions.

Beyond traditional policing, Military Police soldiers specialize in force protection, anti-terrorism, and protective services for senior leaders. The career field offers unique opportunities including criminal investigation with CID, military working dog handling, and special reaction team operations. Many MP skills translate directly to civilian law enforcement careers after service.

Training Pipeline

From Trainee to MP

  • Phase 1 20-Week OSUT — One Station Unit Training at Ft Leonard Wood, MO combines Basic Combat Training and Advanced Individual Training into a single course covering warrior tasks, law enforcement fundamentals, and police operations.
  • Phase 2 Unit Assignment — Report to your first MP unit and begin on-the-job training in garrison law enforcement, patrol operations, and force protection missions.
  • Phase 3 Law Enforcement Certifications — Obtain military law enforcement certifications and specialized training in areas such as traffic accident investigation, evidence collection, and use of force.
  • Phase 4 NCOES — Noncommissioned Officer Education System courses for career progression and leadership development within the MP corps.
  • Phase 5 Advanced Assignments — Opportunities for specialization in CID, K-9, protective services, or special reaction teams based on performance and career goals.
Advanced Training

Specialized Courses

Military Police soldiers can attend a variety of advanced courses to sharpen their expertise: CID Special Agent Course for criminal investigation, K-9 Handler Course for military working dog operations, Protective Services training for VIP security details, and SRT (Special Reaction Team) qualification for high-risk tactical law enforcement operations.
