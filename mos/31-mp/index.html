Career Field at a Glance

20-Week OSUT — 3+ MOS — 1 Mission: Law & Order

The 31-Series is the Army's law enforcement and security force. From garrison policing to combat zone security operations, Military Police Soldiers own the mission of maintaining discipline, enforcing the law, and protecting the force.

MOS Specialties
31B
Military Police
OSUT20 Weeks
LocationFort Leonard Wood, MO
ASVABST: 91
DutyCombat Support
Key SkillsLaw Enforcement, Security, Detainee Ops, Route Security

The 31B Military Police is the backbone of Army law enforcement. MPs conduct law enforcement operations on every installation, provide area security in combat zones, execute route clearance and security patrols, and manage detainee operations. The 20-week OSUT at Fort Leonard Wood combines Basic Combat Training with police-specific Advanced Individual Training in a single continuous course.

Training covers warrior tasks, law enforcement fundamentals, patrol procedures, traffic management, use of force, evidence collection, and base security operations. Graduates emerge as certified military law enforcement officers ready for garrison or combat deployment.

  • Law enforcement operations and patrol procedures
  • Base security, access control, and force protection
  • Detainee operations and EPW handling
  • Route security and convoy escort operations
  • Combat support and area security in theater
  • Traffic management and accident investigation
  • Use of force, handcuffing, and suspect apprehension
31D
Criminal Investigation Special Agent
TrainingCID Special Agent Course
LocationFort Leonard Wood, MO
RequiresAdditional CID School
DutyCriminal Investigation
Key SkillsFelony Investigation, Crime Scene, Forensics, Interrogation

The 31D Criminal Investigation Special Agent serves in the Army's Criminal Investigation Division (CID). CID agents investigate felony-level crimes including homicide, sexual assault, fraud, and drug trafficking. They operate with federal law enforcement authority and work independently or in small teams to solve serious crimes affecting the Army community.

Becoming a CID agent requires completion of the CID Special Agent Course in addition to prior MP or law enforcement experience. Agents are trained in advanced interview and interrogation techniques, crime scene processing, digital forensics, and undercover operations.

  • Felony criminal investigations (homicide, assault, fraud)
  • Crime scene processing and evidence collection
  • Interview and interrogation techniques
  • Digital forensics and cyber crime investigation
  • Undercover operations and surveillance
  • Coordination with federal, state, and local law enforcement
  • Court testimony and case file preparation
31E
Internment/Resettlement Specialist
AIT8+ Weeks
LocationFort Leonard Wood, MO
ASVABST: 95
DutyCorrections / Detainee Ops
Key SkillsDetainee Ops, Corrections, EPW Handling, Facility Mgmt

The 31E Internment/Resettlement Specialist manages the Army's detainee operations and corrections facilities. These soldiers are responsible for the custody, care, and control of enemy prisoners of war (EPWs), civilian internees, and detained persons in accordance with the Geneva Conventions and Army regulations.

31Es operate detention facilities, process and classify detainees, maintain facility security, and ensure humane treatment in compliance with international law. The role requires strong discipline, attention to detail, and the ability to manage high-stress custody environments.

  • Detainee operations and processing procedures
  • Corrections facility management and security
  • Enemy prisoner of war (EPW) handling per Geneva Conventions
  • Facility construction and sustainment operations
  • Detainee accountability and records management
  • Use of force and restraint procedures in custody settings
  • Humanitarian assistance and displaced civilian operations
31Z
Senior MP Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE Level
DutyProvost Operations
Key SkillsProvost Ops, Force Protection, Training Mgmt, Leadership

The 31Z Senior MP Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted Military Police leadership. 31Zs serve as operations sergeants major, first sergeants, and command sergeants major at battalion and brigade level. They are responsible for provost operations, force protection planning, and the training and readiness of all MP Soldiers in their formation.

Reaching 31Z requires decades of MP experience, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a proven track record of law enforcement and combat operations leadership across multiple assignments.

  • Provost marshal operations at BN/BDE level
  • Force protection and antiterrorism planning
  • Training management and unit readiness oversight
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Law enforcement policy and standards enforcement
  • Mentorship of junior NCOs and integration with maneuver units
  • Senior enlisted advisor to the Provost Marshal

Junior enlisted MPs begin their career on patrol, working the desk, or manning access control points. New soldiers learn garrison law enforcement, report writing, traffic enforcement, and use-of-force procedures under the mentorship of experienced NCOs. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years.

This is the time to build foundational law enforcement skills, pursue certifications, and volunteer for specialized assignments like K-9, SRT, or protective services details.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Patrol officer, basic law enforcement tasks
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient patrolman, traffic enforcement
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Experienced MP, mentor to new soldiers
  • Pursue Airborne, Air Assault, or specialized MP courses early
  • Volunteer for deployment and special duty assignments
  • Obtain civilian law enforcement certifications when available
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years

SchoolsBLC, ALC, SRT
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER performance

MP NCOs are the first-line leaders of law enforcement and security operations. Sergeants (E-5) lead fire teams and patrol shifts, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) serve as desk sergeants, squad leaders, and operations NCOs. They are directly responsible for the training, discipline, and tactical employment of junior MPs.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Specialized schools like SRT, K-9, and CID significantly enhance career progression.

  • SGT (E-5): Team Leader / Shift Supervisor — leads 3-5 MP soldiers on patrol
  • SSG (E-6): Desk Sergeant / Squad Leader — manages shift operations
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • SRT and K-9 handler qualifications build competitive records
  • CID application opens at E-5 with investigative experience
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsPSG, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior MP NCOs represent the most experienced law enforcement leaders in the Army. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as Platoon Sergeants and Operations NCOs, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve as senior enlisted advisors at battalion, brigade, and provost marshal offices.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A proven record across garrison law enforcement, combat operations, and advanced MP assignments is essential for selection.

  • SFC (E-7): Platoon Sergeant — senior tactical NCO for MP platoon operations
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — company-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Provost sergeant major and drill sergeant duty build competitive records
OCS
Officer Commissioning
PathOCS / Green-to-Gold
Duration12-16 Weeks (OCS)
LocationFort Moore, GA
Result2LT Commission
BranchMP Officer (31A)

Enlisted MP Soldiers with leadership potential and a bachelor's degree can pursue commissioning through Officer Candidate School (OCS) or the Green-to-Gold program. OCS is a 12-week intensive course at Fort Moore, while Green-to-Gold sends soldiers to college full-time with an ROTC scholarship.

Former enlisted MPs who commission as MP Officers (31A) bring invaluable law enforcement and operational experience. MP officers serve as platoon leaders, provost marshals, and eventually command MP battalions and brigades.

  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — rapid commissioning path
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • MP Basic Officer Leader Course (MP-BOLC) follows commissioning
  • Prior enlisted MP experience is a massive advantage in platoon leadership
  • Career progression: PL → XO → CO CDR → Provost Marshal → BN CDR
  • Criminal justice or law enforcement degrees strengthen MP officer careers

Common Questions
Career
Want to Be a Cop?

Military Police is the Army's direct path to law enforcement. You'll earn real-world police experience, certifications, and skills that translate directly to civilian LE careers after service.

Investigation
Interested in CID?

CID Special Agents investigate felony crimes with federal authority. Build your 31B experience, maintain a clean record, and apply for the CID Special Agent Course to become a criminal investigator.

Legal
Struggling with Law?

MP training covers UCMJ, use of force, search and seizure, and evidence law. Master the legal fundamentals early — they define every action you take on patrol and in investigations.

Transition
Need Civilian LE Career?

MP experience is highly valued by federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies. Many MPs transition to FBI, DEA, Secret Service, U.S. Marshals, or local police departments after service.

Specialty
K-9 Handler Questions?

Military Working Dog handlers are a specialized MP assignment. Pursue K-9 handler school after your first duty station — it's one of the most rewarding and competitive assignments in the MP corps.

Promotion
How Fast Can I Promote?

MP promotion to E-5 averages 3-4 years. BLC completion, strong NCOERs, specialized certifications (SRT, K-9, CID), and deployment experience accelerate the timeline significantly.
