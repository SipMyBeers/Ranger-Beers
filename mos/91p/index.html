Artillery Mechanic

The maintenance of the Army's indirect power. Artillery Mechanics maintain and repair the complex systems, engines, and electronics of Army howitzers.

Overview

What You'll Do

As an Artillery Mechanic (91P), you maintain and repair the Army's diverse howitzer platforms. You'll ensure that the engines, recoil systems, and fire control systems are in top condition, ensuring the lethality and readiness of the King of Battle in all environments.

Key Responsibilities

  • Maintain and repair howitzer airframes and engines
  • Inspect and service recoil and fire control systems
  • Troubleshoot weapon platform failures and malfunctions
  • Use specialized tools and diagnostic equipment
  • Ensure compliance with ordnance safety and maintenance standards

Training

Basic Combat Training followed by Advanced Individual Training at Fort Sill, Oklahoma. Specialized training in mechanics, hydraulics, and howitzer-specific systems.
