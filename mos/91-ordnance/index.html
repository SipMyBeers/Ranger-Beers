Junior Enlisted

BonusUp to $20K (initial)

Junior enlisted Ordnance Soldiers begin their career on the shop floor, performing maintenance tasks under the supervision of experienced NCOs. This is where you build your technical foundation — learning to diagnose faults, execute repairs, and master the technical manuals for your assigned platform.

Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years and brings increased responsibility as a lead mechanic or shop specialist. This is the time to earn platform certifications and begin pursuing ASE credentials.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Apprentice mechanic, basic tasks under supervision
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient on assigned vehicle/equipment platform
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Lead mechanic, mentors new soldiers
  • Pursue ASE certifications and platform-specific training early
  • Volunteer for field maintenance teams and deployment support
  • Build technical proficiency for competitive promotion
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsShop Foreman, Section NCOIC
SchoolsBLC, ALC, Tech Inspector
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER + Technical Skill

Ordnance NCOs lead maintenance sections and shops, directly responsible for the readiness of assigned equipment. Sergeants (E-5) serve as shop foremen or team leaders, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) run maintenance sections and serve as quality control inspectors.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Technical Inspector certification and advanced platform training significantly improve promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Shop Foreman — leads 3-5 mechanics in daily operations
  • SSG (E-6): Section NCOIC — manages maintenance section, readiness reporting
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • Technical Inspector Course is the premier certification for Ordnance NCOs
  • Pursue Maintenance Manager Course and ASE Master Technician
  • Strong NCOER evaluations and equipment readiness rates are critical
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsMaint Supervisor, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA, Maint Mgr
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior Ordnance NCOs are the most experienced maintenance leaders in the Army. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as Maintenance Supervisors and Platoon Sergeants, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and higher echelons.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of maintaining high equipment readiness rates, advanced school completion, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential.

  • SFC (E-7): Maintenance Supervisor — oversees shop operations and readiness
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — company-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Maintenance Manager Course and instructor duty build competitive records
WO
Warrant Officer
PathWOCS + WOBC
Duration5 Weeks WOCS + 18 Weeks WOBC
LocationFort Novosel / Ft Gregg-Adams
ResultWO1 → CW5
RoleTechnical Expert & Advisor

The Warrant Officer track is the premier career path for Ordnance Soldiers who want to become the Army's highest-level technical experts. Ordnance Warrant Officers (MOS 913A, 914A, 915A, 919A) serve as maintenance technicians, property book officers, and senior maintenance advisors from company through theater level.

Warrant Officers bridge the gap between NCO expertise and officer authority. They are the subject matter experts that commanders rely on for maintenance program management, equipment fielding, and technical problem-solving. Many Ordnance Soldiers view the Warrant Officer path as the ultimate career goal.

  • WOCS: 5 weeks at Fort Novosel — Warrant Officer Candidate School
  • WOBC: 18 weeks at Fort Gregg-Adams — Warrant Officer Basic Course
  • 913A: Armament Systems Maintenance Warrant Officer
  • 914A: Allied Trades Warrant Officer
  • 915A: Automotive Maintenance Warrant Officer
  • 919A: Engineer Equipment Maintenance Warrant Officer
  • Career progression: WO1 → CW2 → CW3 → CW4 → CW5

Common Questions
Career
Love Fixing Vehicles?

91B Wheeled Vehicle Mechanic is the most common Ordnance MOS. If you love turning wrenches, diagnosing problems, and keeping trucks rolling, this is your career field. Skills transfer directly to civilian automotive careers.

Certification
Need ASE Certification?

The Army supports ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) certification for Ordnance Soldiers. Earn industry-recognized credentials while serving — civilian employers pay a premium for ASE-certified mechanics.

Advancement
Want Warrant Officer?

Ordnance offers one of the best paths to Warrant Officer. Become the Army's top technical expert in maintenance, earn WO1 through CW5 rank, and lead maintenance programs at the highest levels.

MOS
Generator Specialist?

91D Tactical Power Generation Specialists keep the lights on for every command post, TOC, and field operation. Without power, nothing works. It is a critical and always-in-demand specialty.

Transition
Civilian Mechanic Career?

Ordnance skills translate directly to civilian careers in automotive, diesel, HVAC, electrical, and industrial maintenance. Army mechanics are among the most sought-after veterans in the civilian workforce.

Promotion
How Fast Can I Promote?

Ordnance promotion to E-5 averages 3-4 years. BLC completion, strong NCOERs, Technical Inspector certification, and high equipment readiness rates dramatically accelerate the timeline.
