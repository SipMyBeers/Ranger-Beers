Career Field Overview

What is Ordnance?

The Ordnance branch is responsible for maintaining and repairing every piece of equipment the Army owns — from M4 rifles and M240 machine guns to M1 Abrams tanks, Bradley Fighting Vehicles, Strykers, and everything in between. Ordnance Soldiers are the mechanics, welders, machinists, and technical experts who ensure that combat systems are operational and ready to fight. Without Ordnance, the Army's equipment would grind to a halt.

The 91-series is one of the largest career fields in the Army, with over 10 distinct MOS codes covering platform-specific maintenance for virtually every vehicle and weapons system in the inventory. Training varies by MOS and is conducted at multiple installations. The hands-on technical skills learned in Ordnance — automotive repair, hydraulics, electronics, welding — translate directly to high-demand civilian careers in maintenance and manufacturing.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Maintainer

  • Step 1BCT — Complete Basic Combat Training before reporting to your MOS-specific AIT.
  • Step 210-26 Week AIT (Various Locations) — AIT length and location depend on the specific MOS. Training covers vehicle/weapons system theory, diagnostics, repair procedures, and safety. Heavy emphasis on hands-on practical work.
  • Step 3Unit Assignment — Report to a maintenance company, Forward Support Company, or Brigade Support Battalion. Begin performing maintenance on assigned equipment.
  • Step 4Technical Certifications — Earn platform-specific certifications, qualify as a Technical Inspector, or pursue ASE (Automotive Service Excellence) certifications.
  • Step 5NCOES & Advanced Courses — Progress through NCOES. Senior Ordnance NCOs pursue Maintenance Manager Course, Technical Inspector Course, or platform-specific master courses.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Ordnance Soldiers can pursue: Technical Inspector Course, Maintenance Manager Course, platform-specific advanced maintenance courses (Abrams, Bradley, Stryker), ASE certifications, welding certifications, and EOD support training. Senior NCOs attend the Ordnance Senior Leader Course.
