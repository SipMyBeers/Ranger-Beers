Career Field at a Glance

10-Week AIT — 2+ MOS — 1 Mission: Human Resources

The 42-Series is the Army's personnel and administrative services force. From processing awards and evaluations to managing pay and casualty operations, AG Soldiers keep the entire Army running behind the scenes.

MOS Specialties
42A
Human Resources Specialist
AIT10 Weeks
LocationFort Jackson, SC
ASVABGT: 100
DutyCombat Service Support
Key SkillsPersonnel Actions, IPPS-A, Evaluations, Awards, Pay

The 42A Human Resources Specialist is the cornerstone of the Army's personnel management system. Every unit from company to corps depends on 42As to process personnel actions, evaluations (NCOERs/OERs), awards, promotions, pay actions, leaves, and casualty operations. AIT at Fort Jackson trains soldiers on eMILPO/IPPS-A system administration and the full spectrum of HR operations.

42As serve in S-1/G-1 sections at every echelon, managing the administrative backbone that keeps the force paid, promoted, and accounted for. The skills developed — data management, personnel systems, and organizational leadership — translate directly to civilian HR and management careers.

  • Personnel actions processing (accessions, separations, reassignments)
  • Evaluation management (NCOERs, OERs, AERs)
  • Awards and decorations processing
  • Promotions, reductions, and board actions
  • Pay, leave, and entitlements administration
  • Casualty operations and notifications
  • eMILPO/IPPS-A system administration and reporting
42S
Special Band Member
EntryAudition-Based
ProgramArmy Band
ASVABGT: 100
DutySpecial Duty
Key SkillsMusical Performance, Ceremonies, Community Engagement

The 42S Special Band Member serves in the Army's premier musical organizations. Entry is audition-based, requiring demonstrated proficiency on a primary instrument. Band members perform at official functions, change-of-command ceremonies, military funerals, community engagement events, and diplomatic occasions representing the Army worldwide.

42S soldiers combine musical excellence with standard military duties. They maintain physical fitness standards, deploy with their units, and serve as ambassadors of the Army at home and abroad. The Army band program offers a unique career path for musically talented soldiers.

  • Performance at official military functions and ceremonies
  • Change-of-command, retirement, and memorial ceremonies
  • Community engagement concerts and public affairs events
  • Diplomatic and protocol performances at senior leader events
  • Musical proficiency maintenance and ensemble rehearsals
  • Deployment support and troop morale performances
  • Recording and production for Army media
42Z
Senior HR Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE/DIV Level
DutySenior Enlisted Leader
Key SkillsS-1/G-1 Ops, Personnel Mgmt, Policy, Mentorship

The 42Z Senior HR Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted AG leadership. 42Zs serve as S-1/G-1 NCOICs and operations sergeants major at battalion, brigade, and division level. They are responsible for all personnel management, HR systems oversight, and administrative policy execution across their organization.

Reaching 42Z requires years of HR experience, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a proven record of excellence in personnel management across multiple echelons and duty positions.

  • S-1/G-1 operations management at BN/BDE/DIV level
  • Personnel readiness reporting and strength management
  • Policy development and HR program execution
  • Casualty operations oversight and quality control
  • IPPS-A system management and training oversight
  • Mentorship of junior HR NCOs and soldier development
  • Senior enlisted advisor for personnel and administrative matters

Junior Enlisted

Junior enlisted AG Soldiers begin their career in S-1 sections, learning the fundamentals of personnel administration, awards processing, and HR system operation. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years and brings increased responsibility managing specific personnel programs.

This is the time to master IPPS-A, build expertise in evaluation and awards processing, and develop the administrative precision that defines successful AG professionals.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — HR clerk, basic personnel actions
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Awards processing, leave management
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — NCOER/OER processing, promotions actions
  • Master IPPS-A and eMILPO systems early
  • Volunteer for deployment and personnel accountability missions
  • Build a reputation for accuracy and attention to detail
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsHR NCO, S-1 NCOIC
SchoolsBLC, ALC, AG School
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER performance

AG NCOs are the driving force behind unit-level HR operations. Sergeants (E-5) supervise HR clerks and manage specific programs, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) serve as S-1 NCOICs at company and battalion level, responsible for all personnel actions, evaluations, and administrative readiness.

Completion of BLC (Basic Leader Course) is required for E-5, and ALC (Advanced Leader Course) for E-6. Additional AG-specific functional courses and IPPS-A certification strengthen promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): HR NCO — supervises personnel actions and awards programs
  • SSG (E-6): S-1 NCOIC — manages all HR operations for the unit
  • BLC required for promotion to SGT, ALC for SSG
  • AG-specific functional courses at Fort Jackson
  • Pursue casualty operations and postal management certifications
  • Strong NCOER evaluations are critical for competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsS-1 NCOIC, G-1 SGM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior AG NCOs represent the most experienced personnel managers in the Army. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as battalion S-1 NCOICs, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) manage HR operations at brigade level, and Sergeants Major (E-9) serve as G-1 SGMs at division and higher.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of flawless personnel management, advanced AG school completion, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential for selection.

  • SFC (E-7): BN S-1 NCOIC — manages all battalion HR operations
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): BDE S-1 NCOIC or First Sergeant — brigade-level HR leadership
  • SGM (E-9): G-1 Sergeant Major — DIV/Corps senior HR enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Instructor duty at AG School builds competitive records
AG
AG Branch Officer
PathOCS / ROTC / USMA
DurationAG BOLC ~16 Weeks
LocationFort Jackson, SC
ResultAG Branch Commission
BonusBranch selection

AG Branch officers lead human resources operations from platoon through Army-level organizations. Officers branch AG through OCS, ROTC, or West Point, then attend the AG Basic Officer Leader Course (BOLC) at Fort Jackson. AG officers manage S-1/G-1 sections, personnel policies, and strategic human capital programs.

Prior enlisted AG soldiers who commission bring invaluable HR system expertise and personnel management experience that makes them exceptionally effective as S-1 officers and G-1 staff members.

  • AG BOLC: ~16 weeks at Fort Jackson — HR officer foundation
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • S-1 OIC at company and battalion level
  • G-1 staff positions at brigade, division, and corps
  • Prior enlisted experience is a significant advantage in HR leadership
  • Career progression: S-1 OIC → BN S-1 → BDE S-1 → G-1 Staff → G-1

Common Questions
Career
Good with Admin?

If you're detail-oriented and organized, the AG Corps is a natural fit. HR Specialists manage the paperwork and systems that keep the entire Army functioning — and those skills are in demand everywhere.

Systems
Know IPPS-A?

IPPS-A is the Army's integrated personnel and pay system. Mastering it early makes you indispensable in any S-1 section and sets you apart for promotion and special assignments.

Training
Struggling with Awards?

Awards processing is one of the most visible tasks in HR. Learn AR 600-8-22 inside and out, master the DA Form 638, and become the go-to expert in your section for decorations.

Knowledge
How Do Evaluations Work?

NCOERs and OERs are the lifeblood of Army career progression. Understanding evaluation timelines, rating schemes, and AR 623-3 makes you invaluable to your unit's leadership.

Promotion
Want S-1 NCOIC?

The S-1 NCOIC is the most critical enlisted HR position in any battalion. Build expertise in every personnel program, earn top-block NCOERs, and complete ALC to be competitive.

Transition
Civilian HR Career?

AG experience translates directly to civilian HR management, payroll administration, and organizational leadership. PHR/SPHR certifications and a degree in HR make the transition seamless.
