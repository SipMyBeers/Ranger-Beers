Career Field Overview

The Army's HR Backbone

The Adjutant General Corps is the backbone of Army human resources. The 42A Human Resources Specialist handles everything from personnel actions and evaluations to awards, promotions, and separations. Every unit in the Army relies on AG soldiers to keep personnel records accurate, process pay actions, and ensure soldiers and their families receive the support they need. It is one of the most universally needed MOS in the force.

Beyond day-to-day personnel administration, AG soldiers manage casualty operations, postal services, and personnel accountability during deployments and emergencies. They work with automated HR systems like iPERMS and eMILPO to maintain the digital backbone of Army personnel management. The skills developed in this career field — data management, administrative operations, and organizational leadership — translate directly to civilian HR and management careers.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to HR Professional

  • Phase 1 Basic Combat Training — 10 weeks of BCT covering warrior tasks, physical fitness, and fundamental soldiering skills.
  • Phase 2 9-Week AIT — Advanced Individual Training at Ft Jackson, SC. Learn personnel administration, HR systems, correspondence, and records management.
  • Phase 3 Unit Assignment — Report to your first unit and begin processing personnel actions, awards, evaluations, and maintaining soldier records.
  • Phase 4 HR Systems Training — Gain proficiency in Army HR systems including iPERMS, eMILPO, and other personnel management platforms.
  • Phase 5 NCOES — Noncommissioned Officer Education System courses for leadership development and career progression within the AG Corps.
  • Phase 6 Senior HR Management — Advanced positions in brigade and division-level HR operations, casualty management, and strategic personnel planning.
Advanced Training

Specialized Courses

AG soldiers can pursue advanced training in Postal Operations for military mail management, Casualty Operations for notification and assistance procedures, HR Systems certification for iPERMS and eMILPO administration, and the Senior Leader HR Course for advanced personnel management at higher echelons.
