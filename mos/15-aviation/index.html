Career Field Overview

What is Army Aviation?

The Aviation branch maintains and operates the Army's fleet of over 4,000 aircraft, including the AH-64 Apache attack helicopter, UH-60 Black Hawk utility helicopter, CH-47 Chinook heavy-lift helicopter, and a growing fleet of unmanned aircraft systems. Aviation Soldiers serve as mechanics, avionics technicians, crew chiefs, and UAS operators, ensuring that Army aircraft are safe and mission-capable around the clock.

The 15-series encompasses one of the largest career fields in the Army, with over 15 distinct MOS codes covering everything from powerplant repair to aircraft structural maintenance to unmanned systems operation. Training is conducted primarily at Ft Novosel (formerly Ft Rucker), Alabama — the Home of Army Aviation. The technical skills learned in Aviation translate directly to high-paying civilian careers in the aerospace industry.

Training Pipeline

From Recruit to Aviation Specialist

  • Step 1BCT — Complete Basic Combat Training at one of the Army's BCT installations before moving to Aviation AIT.
  • Step 215-23 Week AIT at Ft Novosel, AL — Advanced Individual Training covers aircraft-specific maintenance, safety procedures, technical manuals, and hands-on repair skills. Length varies by MOS.
  • Step 3Unit Assignment — Report to a Combat Aviation Brigade, General Support Aviation Battalion, or other aviation unit. Begin on-the-job training and aircraft qualification.
  • Step 4Aircraft-Specific Qualifications — Earn certifications for specific airframes and systems. Progress toward Crew Chief, Technical Inspector, or maintenance supervisor roles.
  • Step 5NCOES & Advanced Schools — Attend NCO Education System courses. Senior Aviation NCOs may pursue Flight School (Warrant Officer), Instructor Pilot, Aviation Safety, or Maintenance Test Pilot programs.
Advanced Training

Schools & Courses

Aviation Soldiers can pursue: Aircraft-specific advanced maintenance courses, Flight School (for Warrant Officer candidates), Instructor Pilot Course, Aviation Safety Officer Course, Maintenance Test Pilot Course, UAS Master Trainer, and FAA certifications including A&P (Airframe & Powerplant) mechanic licensure.
