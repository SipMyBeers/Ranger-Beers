Career Field at a Glance

15-23 Week AIT — 10+ MOS — 1 Mission: Army Aviation

The 15-Series is one of the Army's largest and most technically demanding career fields. From Black Hawks to Apaches to Gray Eagles, Aviation Soldiers keep the fleet flying and the mission moving.

MOS Specialties
15T
UH-60 Black Hawk Helicopter Repairer
AIT15 Weeks
LocationFort Novosel, AL
ASVABMM: 104
DutyAviation Maintenance
Key SkillsMaintenance, Inspections, Phase Mx, Component Repair

The 15T UH-60 Black Hawk Helicopter Repairer is responsible for maintaining the Army's workhorse utility helicopter. Black Hawks serve in every Aviation unit across the force — medevac, assault, command and control, and special operations. 15Ts perform scheduled and unscheduled maintenance, troubleshoot mechanical and hydraulic systems, and ensure aircraft are safe for flight.

AIT at Fort Novosel covers airframe systems, powerplant operations, rotor and drive train maintenance, and aviation ground support equipment. Graduates report to Combat Aviation Brigades, General Support Aviation Battalions, and Medevac units worldwide.

  • Scheduled and unscheduled maintenance on UH-60A/L/M airframes
  • Phase maintenance inspections and component-level repair
  • Rotor head, drive train, and flight control system servicing
  • Engine run-up, ground handling, and pre-flight/post-flight inspections
  • Aviation ground support equipment (AGSE) operation
  • Technical manual interpretation and maintenance documentation
  • Crew Chief progression — fly as part of the aircrew
15R
AH-64 Apache Attack Helicopter Repairer
AIT15 Weeks
LocationFort Novosel, AL
ASVABMM: 104
DutyAttack Aviation Mx
Key SkillsAttack Helo Mx, Weapons Systems, Avionics

The 15R AH-64 Apache Attack Helicopter Repairer maintains the Army's premier attack helicopter platform. The Apache is a twin-engine attack helicopter equipped with a 30mm chain gun, Hellfire missiles, and advanced avionics — and 15Rs keep it lethal and mission-capable. This MOS demands strong mechanical aptitude and the ability to troubleshoot complex weapons and avionics systems.

15Rs work in Attack Reconnaissance Battalions and Squadrons, performing maintenance on airframe, armament, and electronic systems. The technical skills translate directly to defense contractor and aerospace careers.

  • AH-64D/E Apache airframe maintenance and inspections
  • Weapons systems maintenance — M230 chain gun, Hellfire, Hydra 70
  • Avionics troubleshooting and line replaceable unit (LRU) swap
  • TADS/PNVS (Target Acquisition / Pilot Night Vision) system servicing
  • Engine, transmission, and rotor system maintenance
  • Integrated vehicle health management system (IVHMS) diagnostics
  • Phase maintenance and aircraft battle damage assessment/repair
15W
Unmanned Aircraft Systems Operator
AIT23 Weeks
LocationFort Novosel, AL
ASVABST: 101
DutyUAS Operations
Key SkillsUAS Ops, Mission Planning, Sensor Ops

The 15W Unmanned Aircraft Systems Operator is one of the fastest-growing specialties in Army Aviation. 15Ws operate the Army's fleet of unmanned aerial vehicles, including the MQ-1C Gray Eagle and RQ-7B Shadow, providing real-time intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR) to commanders on the battlefield. This MOS sits at the intersection of aviation and intelligence.

AIT is the longest in the 15-Series at 23 weeks, covering UAS flight operations, mission planning, sensor payload operation, airspace coordination, and tactical employment. Graduates serve in UAS companies organic to Combat Aviation Brigades and Military Intelligence Battalions.

  • MQ-1C Gray Eagle and RQ-7B Shadow flight operations
  • Mission planning, route development, and airspace deconfliction
  • EO/IR sensor and SAR payload operation and target acquisition
  • Launch, recovery, and ground control station (GCS) procedures
  • Real-time ISR feed dissemination to supported units
  • Emergency procedures, lost link protocols, and flight safety
  • Integration with brigade-level intelligence and fires cells
15Z
Senior Aviation Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeBN/BDE Level
DutyAviation Mx Management
Key SkillsReadiness Reporting, Maintenance Mgmt, Leadership

The 15Z Senior Aviation Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted Aviation leadership. 15Zs serve as production control NCOICs, aviation maintenance technicians, first sergeants, and command sergeants major at battalion and brigade level. They are responsible for aircraft readiness reporting, maintenance management, and the training and welfare of all Aviation enlisted personnel.

Reaching 15Z requires decades of aviation maintenance experience across multiple airframes, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a proven record of maintaining high operational readiness rates across the fleet.

  • Aviation maintenance management and production control oversight
  • Unit readiness reporting and aircraft availability tracking
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling
  • Quality assurance and Technical Inspector program supervision
  • Safety program management and accident prevention
  • Mentorship of junior NCOs across all 15-series specialties
  • Senior enlisted advisor for aviation operations at BN/BDE level

E1-E4
Junior Enlisted
Timeline0-4 Years

SchoolsAirborne, Air Assault

BonusUp to $40K (initial)

Junior enlisted Aviation Soldiers begin on the flight line learning hands-on maintenance under the supervision of experienced NCOs and warrant officers. Expect long hours on the hangar floor, learning technical manuals, and building proficiency on your assigned airframe. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years.

This is the time to earn your Crew Chief wings, pursue additional certifications, and build the technical foundation that will define your Aviation career. Aviation MOS qualifies soldiers for FAA Airframe & Powerplant (A&P) certification — start tracking your hours early.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — apprentice mechanic, basic tasks under supervision
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — proficient maintainer, pre-flight/post-flight qualified
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Crew Chief qualified, mentor to new soldiers
  • Pursue Air Assault and Airborne qualification early
  • Begin tracking maintenance hours toward FAA A&P certification
  • Build technical competence for competitive promotion to SGT
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsSection SGT, TI, Prod Control
SchoolsBLC, ALC, AMTC
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER + Technical Skill

Aviation NCOs are the backbone of maintenance operations. Sergeants (E-5) lead maintenance sections and serve as Technical Inspectors, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) manage production control and serve as section leaders responsible for aircraft readiness. Aviation demands both leadership ability and deep technical knowledge.

Completion of BLC is required for E-5, ALC for E-6. Aviation Maintenance Technician Course (AMTC) and advanced airframe-specific courses significantly improve promotion potential and technical credibility.

  • SGT (E-5): Section Sergeant / Technical Inspector — quality assurance on maintenance tasks
  • SSG (E-6): Production Control / Platoon Sergeant — manages maintenance workflow and readiness
  • BLC required for SGT, ALC for SSG promotion
  • Technical Inspector (TI) certification is a career-defining qualification
  • Pursue advanced airframe courses and instructor duty
  • Strong NCOER evaluations and readiness metrics drive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsMaint Tech, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior Aviation NCOs represent the most experienced maintainers and leaders in the branch. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as aviation maintenance technicians and platoon sergeants, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and higher as the senior enlisted advisor for all aviation operations.

These positions are highly competitive and board-selected. A record of high aircraft readiness rates, advanced school completion, and consistent top-block evaluations are essential for selection.

  • SFC (E-7): Aviation Maintenance Technician — senior technical authority for maintenance operations
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): First Sergeant or Operations NCOIC — company-level senior enlisted
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Aviation Safety Course and instructor duty build competitive records
WOFT
Warrant Officer Flight Training
PathWOFT / Street-to-Seat
Duration~18 Months
LocationFort Novosel, AL
ResultWO1 Aviator
UniqueNo degree required

Aviation offers a unique career path found in no other branch: the Warrant Officer Flight Training (WOFT) program. Enlisted Aviation Soldiers — and even civilians through the "Street-to-Seat" program — can apply to become Army helicopter pilots without a college degree. This is the only commissioning-equivalent path in the military that does not require a bachelor's degree.

WOFT is approximately 18 months of intensive flight training at Fort Novosel, progressing from the TH-67 trainer through advanced airframe qualification in Apache, Black Hawk, or Chinook. Warrant Officer Aviators serve as the Army's primary helicopter pilots and technical aviation experts throughout their career.

  • WOCS: 5 weeks of Warrant Officer Candidate School at Fort Novosel
  • IERW: Initial Entry Rotary Wing — primary flight training and instrument qualification
  • Advanced airframe track: AH-64 Apache, UH-60 Black Hawk, or CH-47 Chinook
  • Street-to-Seat: civilians can apply directly — no prior military service required
  • Prior enlisted maintainers have a significant advantage in the selection process
  • Career progression: WO1 → CW2 → CW3 → CW4 → CW5 (Master Aviator)

Common Questions
Career
Want to Work on Helicopters?

Aviation AIT trains you hands-on with the Army's rotary-wing fleet. From Black Hawks to Apaches to Chinooks, 15-series maintainers keep the aircraft mission-ready and earn skills that translate directly to civilian aerospace careers.

UAS
Interested in UAS?

15W Unmanned Aircraft Systems Operators fly Gray Eagle and Shadow drones, providing real-time ISR to commanders on the battlefield. It's the fastest-growing specialty in Aviation with massive civilian demand.

Flight
How Do I Become a Pilot?

The Warrant Officer Flight Training (WOFT) program lets enlisted soldiers — and even civilians — become Army helicopter pilots without a college degree. Prior 15-series experience gives you a real edge in the selection process.

Certification
Need A&P License?

Army Aviation maintenance hours count toward your FAA Airframe & Powerplant (A&P) mechanic certification. Start tracking hours from Day 1 — the A&P license is your ticket to six-figure civilian aviation jobs.

Medical
Flight Medic Questions?

68W Combat Medics can earn the Flight Medic (F) identifier and serve on medevac crews alongside 15T Black Hawk maintainers. It's one of the most rewarding and high-demand specialties in Army Aviation.

Promotion
How Fast Can I Promote?

Aviation promotion to E-5 averages 3-4 years. BLC completion, strong NCOERs, Technical Inspector certification, and airframe-specific advanced courses accelerate the timeline significantly.
