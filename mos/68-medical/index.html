Career Field Overview

Saving Lives on Every Front

The Army Medical Department (AMEDD) enlisted force is one of the largest and most diverse career fields in the military. The 68-Series encompasses over 20 specialties spanning the full spectrum of healthcare — from the iconic 68W Combat Medic who provides lifesaving care on the battlefield to operating room specialists, dental technicians, radiology techs, pharmacy specialists, and behavioral health professionals. Army medics serve in every type of unit, from infantry platoons to world-class military hospitals.

Medical MOS training is centered at the Medical Education and Training Campus (METC) at Ft Sam Houston, TX, where soldiers receive clinical instruction that often leads to nationally recognized certifications. Many 68-Series soldiers earn credentials as EMTs, paramedics, licensed practical nurses, or certified surgical technologists during their military training. The medical career field offers some of the strongest civilian career translation in the Army, with graduates entering healthcare careers at every level.

Training Pipeline

From Trainee to Healthcare Professional

  • Phase 1 Basic Combat Training — 10 weeks of BCT covering warrior tasks, physical fitness, and fundamental soldiering skills.
  • Phase 2 16-52 Week AIT — Advanced Individual Training at Ft Sam Houston, TX (METC). Training length varies significantly by specialty, with clinical rotations and nationally recognized certification exams.
  • Phase 3 Unit Assignment — Report to your first unit — whether a line infantry company, a combat support hospital, or a military treatment facility — and begin applying clinical skills.
  • Phase 4 Clinical Certifications — Obtain and maintain nationally recognized clinical certifications such as EMT, NREMT, LPN, or specialty-specific credentials.
  • Phase 5 NCOES — Noncommissioned Officer Education System courses for leadership development and career progression within Army Medicine.
  • Phase 6 Advanced Training — Opportunities for flight medic, special operations medical training, critical care, and advanced clinical specialization.
Advanced Training

Specialized Courses

Medical soldiers have access to some of the Army's most prestigious advanced courses: Flight Medic qualification for aeromedical evacuation, SOCM (Special Operations Combat Medic) for SOF support, EFMB (Expert Field Medical Badge) testing, Paramedic certification, Critical Care training for ICU-level patient management, and the Interservice Physician Assistant Program (IPAP) for enlisted soldiers pursuing a PA degree and officer commission.
