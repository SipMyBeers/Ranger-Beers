Career Field at a Glance

16-52 Week AIT — 20+ MOS — 1 Mission: Save Lives

The 68-Series is the Army's largest career field. From the iconic Combat Medic to operating room specialists, practical nurses, and senior medical NCOs, AMEDD enlisted soldiers deliver healthcare across the full spectrum of military operations.

MOS Specialties
68W
Combat Medic Specialist
AIT16 Weeks
LocationFort Sam Houston, TX (METC)
CertEMT-B (NREMT)
DutyCombat Support
Key SkillsTCCC, Trauma, Sick Call, Field Medicine

The 68W Combat Medic Specialist is the most iconic medical MOS in the Army. Whiskeys serve at every echelon of care, from the infantry platoon aid bag to battalion aid stations and combat support hospitals. The 16-week AIT at Fort Sam Houston's Medical Education and Training Campus (METC) produces nationally certified EMT-Bs who are trained in tactical combat casualty care (TCCC), trauma management, and primary care.

68Ws are embedded with line units across every branch and serve as the first responder on the battlefield. They are the most widely assigned MOS in the Army Medical Department and often the difference between life and death for wounded soldiers.

  • Tactical Combat Casualty Care (TCCC) — care under fire, tactical field care, CASEVAC
  • EMT-Basic certification through NREMT national exam
  • Trauma assessment, hemorrhage control, airway management
  • IV therapy, medication administration, and pharmacology
  • Sick call procedures, primary care screening, and triage
  • Field sanitation, preventive medicine, and force health protection
  • Clinical rotations in emergency departments and ambulance services
68C
Practical Nursing Specialist
AIT52 Weeks
LocationFort Sam Houston, TX (METC)
CertLPN (NCLEX-PN)
DutyClinical Nursing
Key SkillsPatient Care, Meds, Clinical Rotations

The 68C Practical Nursing Specialist completes the longest AIT in the 68-Series at 52 weeks, emerging as a Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) eligible to sit for the NCLEX-PN exam. 68Cs provide direct patient care in military treatment facilities, combat support hospitals, and field medical units worldwide.

Training covers the full scope of practical nursing: patient assessment, medication administration, wound care, IV therapy, and clinical documentation. Extensive clinical rotations in hospital wards, surgical units, and outpatient clinics prepare 68Cs for independent patient care under registered nurse supervision.

  • LPN certification — nationally recognized credential through NCLEX-PN
  • Patient assessment, vital signs monitoring, and documentation
  • Medication administration including IV push, IM, and oral routes
  • Wound care, dressing changes, and infection control
  • Clinical rotations in med-surg, OB, pediatrics, and psychiatry
  • Care planning and coordination with RN and physician teams
  • Combat nursing operations in field and CSH environments
68D
Operating Room Specialist
AIT24 Weeks
LocationFort Sam Houston, TX (METC)
CertCertified Surgical Tech
DutySurgical Support
Key SkillsSterile Technique, OR Procedures, Instruments

The 68D Operating Room Specialist is the surgical technologist of the Army Medical Department. 68Ds assist surgeons during operations by preparing the sterile field, handling instruments, and maintaining surgical equipment. They serve in hospital operating rooms, forward surgical teams, and combat support hospitals where trauma surgery saves lives.

Training at METC covers surgical anatomy, sterile technique, instrument identification, and hands-on clinical rotations in operating rooms. Graduates are eligible for national certification as a Certified Surgical Technologist (CST).

  • Surgical technology fundamentals and operating room protocols
  • Sterile technique, gowning, gloving, and field maintenance
  • Surgical instrument identification, use, and management
  • Patient positioning, prepping, and draping for surgery
  • Hemostasis, tissue handling, and suture/staple assistance
  • Clinical rotations in general surgery, orthopedics, and trauma
  • Forward surgical team (FST) operations in austere environments
68Z
Senior Medical Sergeant
RankE-8 / E-9
RequiresSLC + Selection
ScopeClinic / Hospital NCOIC
DutyMedical Operations Mgmt
Key SkillsMed Ops, Training Mgmt, Mentorship

The 68Z Senior Medical Sergeant is the pinnacle of enlisted medical leadership. 68Zs serve as clinic NCOICs, hospital section leaders, and medical operations managers at battalion, brigade, and hospital levels. They are responsible for medical readiness, personnel management, and clinical standards across Army Medicine.

Reaching 68Z requires years of medical experience across multiple specialties, completion of the Senior Leader Course and Sergeants Major Academy, and a proven record of clinical excellence and leadership in both garrison and deployed environments.

  • Medical operations management at clinic and hospital level
  • Training management and medical readiness reporting
  • Personnel management, evaluations, and career counseling for medical soldiers
  • Clinical standards oversight and quality assurance
  • Unit medical readiness and force health protection programs
  • Mentorship of junior medical NCOs across all 68-Series specialties
  • Senior enlisted medical advisor to commanders at BN/BDE level
Career Progression
E1-E4
Junior Enlisted
Timeline0-4 Years
PositionsMedic, Tech, Aide
SchoolsAirborne, Flight Medic
Pay$24K-$36K/yr
BonusUp to $40K (initial)

Junior enlisted medical soldiers begin their career providing direct patient care or clinical support under supervision. 68Ws deploy with line units as platoon medics, while other specialties work in clinics, labs, and hospitals. Promotion to SPC/E-4 typically occurs within 2-3 years and brings increased clinical autonomy.

This is the time to earn additional certifications, pursue advanced medical schools, and build the clinical foundation for NCO leadership. Medical MOS soldiers have access to some of the Army's best continuing education opportunities.

  • PVT (E-1) to PV2 (E-2): 6 months — Basic clinical duties, supervised patient care
  • PFC (E-3): 12 months TIS — Proficient technician, independent clinical tasks
  • SPC (E-4): 24 months TIS — Experienced medic/tech, mentor to new arrivals
  • Pursue advanced certifications (Paramedic, ACLS, PHTLS)
  • Volunteer for Airborne, Flight Medic, or special duty assignments
  • Maintain and upgrade clinical certifications for promotion competitiveness
E5-E6
Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline4-10 Years
PositionsSenior Medic, Clinic NCOIC
SchoolsBLC, ALC, EFMB
Pay$36K-$52K/yr
KeyNCOER + Certifications

Medical NCOs lead clinical teams and manage patient care operations. Sergeants (E-5) serve as senior medics, shift leaders, or clinic supervisors, while Staff Sergeants (E-6) manage sections and mentor junior medical soldiers. This is where medical leaders are forged — balancing clinical excellence with soldier development.

Completion of BLC is required for E-5, and ALC for E-6. The Expert Field Medical Badge (EFMB) is the premier recognition for medical NCOs and significantly improves promotion potential.

  • SGT (E-5): Senior Medic / Shift Leader — oversees clinical operations and junior soldiers
  • SSG (E-6): Section NCOIC — manages clinic sections, training, and medical readiness
  • BLC required for SGT promotion, ALC for SSG
  • Expert Field Medical Badge (EFMB) is the gold standard for medical NCOs
  • Pursue Flight Medic, SOCM, or Critical Care certification
  • Strong NCOER evaluations and maintained certifications drive competitive promotion
E7-E9
Senior Noncommissioned Officer
Timeline10-30 Years
PositionsClinic NCOIC, 1SG, CSM
SchoolsSLC, USASMA
Pay$52K-$90K+/yr
SelectionBoard-selected

Senior medical NCOs represent the most experienced clinical and administrative leaders in Army Medicine. Sergeants First Class (E-7) serve as clinic NCOICs and medical platoon sergeants, Master Sergeants/First Sergeants (E-8) lead at company and hospital section level, and Sergeants Major/Command Sergeants Major (E-9) serve at battalion, brigade, and medical center level.

These positions are board-selected and require a proven record of clinical leadership, advanced school completion, and top-block evaluations across both garrison and deployed medical operations.

  • SFC (E-7): Medical Platoon Sergeant / Clinic NCOIC — senior clinical NCO
  • MSG/1SG (E-8): Operations NCOIC or First Sergeant — company/hospital section leader
  • SGM/CSM (E-9): Command Sergeant Major — BN/BDE/Medical Center senior enlisted advisor
  • Senior Leader Course (SLC) required for E-7 promotion
  • Sergeants Major Academy for E-9 selection
  • Instructor and drill sergeant duty build competitive records
IPAP
Physician Assistant Program
PathIPAP / OCS / Green-to-Gold
Duration29 Months (IPAP)
LocationFort Sam Houston, TX
ResultPA-C / Officer Commission
DegreeMaster's in PA Studies

The Interservice Physician Assistant Program (IPAP) is the premier commissioning pathway for enlisted medical soldiers. IPAP is a 29-month program at Fort Sam Houston that produces Physician Assistants with a master's degree and officer commission. Applicants must be active-duty enlisted with strong clinical experience and prerequisite coursework.

IPAP graduates serve as PA-Cs across the military healthcare system, providing primary care, emergency medicine, and surgical support. Former enlisted medics who complete IPAP bring invaluable clinical experience that sets them apart from their peers.

  • IPAP: 29-month program at Fort Sam Houston — master's degree + PA-C certification
  • OCS: 12-16 weeks at Fort Moore — rapid commissioning for degree holders
  • Green-to-Gold: 2-4 year college program with ROTC scholarship
  • AMEDD direct commission opportunities for qualified clinical professionals
  • Prior enlisted medical experience is a massive advantage in clinical leadership
  • Career progression: PA → Senior PA → Clinic Director → DCCS → Hospital leadership

Common Questions
Career
Want to Be a Combat Medic?

68W is the most widely assigned MOS in the Army. 16 weeks at Fort Sam Houston earns you EMT-B certification and the skills to save lives under fire. Every unit needs a medic — you'll never lack for purpose.

Certification
Need EMT Certification?

68W AIT includes NREMT-B certification as part of the course. You'll graduate with a nationally recognized EMT credential that translates directly to civilian emergency medical services.

Advanced
Interested in Flight Medic?

Flight Medic qualification opens the door to aeromedical evacuation on Black Hawks and Chinooks. It requires paramedic certification and critical care training — the pinnacle of tactical medicine.

Training
How Long is 68C AIT?

68C Practical Nursing AIT is 52 weeks — the longest in the 68-Series. You'll graduate as a Licensed Practical Nurse with LPN credentials and extensive clinical rotation experience.

SOF
SOCM vs Regular Medic?

Special Operations Combat Medic (SOCM) training at Fort Liberty produces tactical medicine experts for SOF units. It's significantly harder than standard 68W AIT and requires SOF selection first.

Transition
Civilian Healthcare Career?

The 68-Series offers the strongest civilian career translation in the Army. Medics become paramedics, nurses, PAs, and healthcare administrators. Your military certifications transfer directly.
