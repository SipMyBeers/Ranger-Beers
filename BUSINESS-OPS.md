## STEP 1: Business Setup (Do This First)

### 1.1 — Form Your Business Entity
1. **Register an LLC** in your state (~$50–$500 depending on state)
2. **Get an EIN** (free) at [irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online)
3. **Get a general business license** from your city/county ($25–$100/year)

### 1.2 — Get Your Resale Certificate
1. Apply for a **Sales Tax Permit** (also called Seller's Permit) from your state's Department of Revenue
2. Usually free or under $25
3. This lets you buy wholesale **tax-free** — you collect sales tax from the end customer instead
4. **Every wholesale supplier will require this** before approving your account

### 1.3 — Open a Business Bank Account
1. Separate personal and business finances from day one
2. Most banks offer free business checking for LLCs

## STEP 3: Choose Your Business Model

### Recommended: Hybrid Model (Self-Fulfill High-Margin + Dropship the Rest)

**Why hybrid wins for ~80 SKUs:**
- Stock items where YOU add value (kits, bundles, laminated cards) = highest margins
- Dropship commodity items and slow movers = zero inventory risk
- Start 60–70% dropship / 30–40% self-fulfill, then shift as you learn what sells

### What to STOCK (Self-Fulfill)

These items have the best margins or require your hands-on assembly:

| Category | Products | Why Stock |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Assembled Kits** | TMK kits, trauma card sets, packing bundles | You build the kit — no one else has your exact config, 50–70% margins |
| **Laminated Cards** | ACE cards, range cards, sector sketches, OPORD boards | DIY cost: $0.10–$0.15/card, sell for $3–$5 each = **90%+ margin** |
| **Repackaged Bulk** | Ear pro 10-packs, golf tees 50-packs, razor kits | Buy in bulk, repackage into field-ready quantities |
| **High-Margin Surplus** | USGI wet weather bags ($2.19 cost → $8.99 sell) | 75–80% margin, lightweight, easy to ship |
| **Lume Tape** | HALO sheets ($1.95/ea → $5–$8 sell) | 60–75% margin, cut-to-size for cat eyes/gear marking |
| **Custom Items** | Patrol cap, waffle bottoms (when ready) | Signature products, no competition |
| **Top 15–20 Sellers** | Track after launch — stock whatever moves fastest | Better margins than dropship + faster shipping |

### What to DROPSHIP

| Category | Products | Why Dropship |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| **Rothco Catalog** | Ponchos, paracord, beanies, boot laces, ECWCS layers | Rothco offers blind drop ship (extra fee), 24-hour fulfillment |
| **Boots** | Belleville, Rocky, Garmont | Bulky, expensive to stock, many sizes |
| **Watches/Compasses** | Casio G-Shock, Cammenga | Slow movers, high unit cost |
| **Books** | Ranger Handbook, Medic Handbook | Bulk Book Store or Amazon dropship |
| **Commodity Items** | Batteries, soap, gum, shaving kits | Low margin, easy to source on demand |

## ANSWER: Buy Bulk vs. Dropship — Which Is Best?

### Short Answer: **Both. Use a hybrid model.**

### The Real Answer:

**Buy bulk and prep yourself** for:
- Anything you **assemble or customize** (TMK kits, laminated cards, lume tape cut-to-size)
- Items with **60%+ margins** when self-fulfilled (wet weather bags, ear pro, golf tees)
- Your **top 15–20 sellers** once you have sales data
- Items that are **small, lightweight, and cheap to store**

**Dropship** for:
- Items you're **testing** — dropship first, stock winners later
- **Bulky/expensive** items (boots, packs) with many size variants
- **Slow movers** that tie up cash sitting on shelves
- **Commodity items** where you can't differentiate

**Never go 100% dropship** — your best margins and brand differentiation come from the items you touch, assemble, and add value to. The TMK kit assembled from $10–$15 in parts selling for $34.99 is infinitely more defensible than reselling the same Rothco beanie as every other dealer.

**Never go 100% self-fulfill at launch** — you don't know what sells yet. Let dropship data tell you what to stock.

## Zoho Mail Setup Guide

Professional email using your custom domain (e.g., info@rangerbeers.com).

### Why Zoho Mail
- Free plan: 5 users, 5GB per user
- Custom domain email (looks professional vs. Gmail)
- No ads on free tier
- Calendar, contacts, and tasks included

### Setup Steps

1. **Sign up** at [zoho.com/mail](https://www.zoho.com/mail/) — choose "Mail Only" free plan
2. **Add your custom domain** (e.g., rangerbeers.com) during setup
3. **Verify domain ownership** — Zoho will provide a TXT record to add to your DNS:
   - Go to your domain registrar (Namecheap, GoDaddy, Cloudflare, etc.)
   - Add a TXT record: `zb{verification-code}` to your domain's DNS settings
   - Wait 5-15 minutes for propagation, then click "Verify" in Zoho
4. **Set up MX records** — Replace existing MX records with Zoho's:
   | Priority | Host | Value |
   |----------|------|-------|
   | 10 | @ | mx.zoho.com |
   | 20 | @ | mx2.zoho.com |
   | 50 | @ | mx3.zoho.com |
5. **Add SPF record** (TXT) to prevent spoofing:
   - `v=spf1 include:zoho.com ~all`
6. **Create email addresses:**
   - `info@rangerbeers.com` — general inquiries
   - `support@rangerbeers.com` — customer support
   - `orders@rangerbeers.com` — order-related (optional)
7. **Update site footer** — Replace placeholder contact email on all HTML pages with your new Zoho email
8. **Set up email forwarding** (optional) — Forward to your personal email for now, switch when volume increases

### DNS Propagation
- MX records can take up to 24-48 hours to fully propagate
- Test by sending an email to your new address from a different account
- Use [mxtoolbox.com](https://mxtoolbox.com/) to verify your MX records are correct
