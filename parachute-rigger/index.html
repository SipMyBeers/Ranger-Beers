The T-11 Personnel phase teaches personnel parachute packing fundamentals. Students learn to pack the T-11 personnel parachute and T-11R reserve — the primary parachute systems used by Airborne forces. Every fold, every tuck, every closing loop is done by the numbers.

Inspection procedures are drilled relentlessly. A rigger's signature on the packing data card means they stake their professional reputation — and a jumper's life — on every chute they pack. Students who cannot achieve 100% accuracy on packing standards are recycled or dropped.

Basic sewing and repair skills begin here. Parachute maintenance requires precise hand-sewing techniques on nylon canopy fabric, suspension lines, and harness assemblies.

Phase Topics
  • T-11 personnel parachute packing — step-by-step procedures
  • T-11R reserve parachute packing and inspection
  • Parachute theory — aerodynamics, deployment sequence, malfunctions
  • Pre-jump and post-jump inspection procedures
  • Basic sewing and repair techniques
  • Packing data card documentation and accountability
  • Quality control standards and rigger accountability

Cargo Systems is where riggers learn to rig heavy equipment and supplies for airdrop. The G-12 cargo parachute system is the workhorse — students pack and rig cargo bundles ranging from ammunition resupply to vehicles.

Container Delivery System (CDS) operations, Low-Velocity Airdrop Delivery System (LVADS), and heavy-drop rigging are all covered. A miscalculated weight or improperly rigged load means equipment is destroyed on impact or the aircraft is endangered during extraction.

Students who cannot master the calculations, weight distributions, and rigging configurations are eliminated. Precision matters — every strap, every shackle, every extraction line must be exact.

Cargo Training Events
  • G-12 cargo parachute packing and rigging
  • Container Delivery System (CDS) operations
  • Low-Velocity Airdrop Delivery System (LVADS)
  • Heavy-drop rigging — vehicles and equipment
  • Sling load operations and helicopter hookup
  • Weight and balance calculations for airdrop loads
  • Aircraft loading and extraction procedures

The Aerial Delivery phase integrates every rigging skill into live airdrop operations. Students rig actual cargo for airdrop from military aircraft, execute the drop, and recover and inspect equipment. This is where classroom theory meets real-world consequences.

The quality control jump is the ultimate test. Every rigger must jump a parachute they personally packed. If you don't trust your own work enough to jump it, you have no business packing chutes for other soldiers.

Final practical examinations cover all personnel and cargo systems. Comprehensive written exams test knowledge of every rigging procedure, safety standard, and technical specification. Successful graduates earn the Parachute Rigger Badge and the 92R MOS.

Aerial Delivery Requirements
  • Rig and execute live airdrop operations
  • Complete quality control jump — jump your own packed chute
  • Pass all practical examinations on personnel and cargo systems
  • Pass comprehensive written examinations
  • Demonstrate zero-defect packing standards
  • Complete all recovery and inspection procedures

Don't Be That Rigger Student.

Every failure at Rigger school has a type. We built a fix for each one.

🔎
Bad at Detail Work?

Parachute rigging is measured in millimeters. If you can't focus on small details for hours at a time, every chute you pack is a liability.
🪂
Can't Pack a Chute?

Packing deficiency is the #1 reason students fail. Every fold, every S-roll, every closing loop must be perfect. Practice the sequence until it's muscle memory.
🔍
Weak on Inspections?

Missing a frayed suspension line or worn closing loop during inspection means a malfunction in the sky. Train your eyes to catch what others miss.
🧵
No Sewing Skills?

Riggers sew. Canopy repairs, harness mending, and line replacement all require precise hand-sewing on nylon. Start practicing before you arrive.
🧠
Bad Memory for Steps?

Packing procedures are long, sequential, and must be performed identically every time. If you struggle with memorization, start studying the TMs now.
⚠
Fear of Responsibility?

Your signature means a soldier's life. Every chute you pack carries your name. If that responsibility scares you, this isn't your MOS.
