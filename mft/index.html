Course Overview

What is the MFT Course?

The Master Fitness Trainer (MFT) Course produces unit-level fitness experts who are qualified to design and implement physical readiness training (PRT) programs tailored to their unit's mission requirements. MFTs serve as the commander's primary advisor on all matters related to physical fitness and soldier readiness.

Graduates return to their units with a deep understanding of exercise science, nutrition, injury prevention, and program design. They are responsible for assessing soldiers' fitness levels, designing progressive training programs, and ensuring that physical training directly supports the unit's combat mission.

Course Phases

4 Weeks of Fitness Science

  • Wks 1-2 Exercise Science — Anatomy, physiology, nutrition, injury prevention, kinesiology, and program design principles. Students learn the science behind physical training and how to build progressive, periodized fitness programs.
  • Wks 3-4 Practical Application — Conduct evaluated PRT sessions, assess individual soldiers, design unit-specific fitness programs, teach proper exercise form, and demonstrate the ability to manage a comprehensive unit physical training program.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Candidates must pass a comprehensive written examination covering exercise science fundamentals, including anatomy, physiology, nutrition, and the principles of physical training program design. The exam ensures MFTs have the academic foundation to make informed training decisions.

The practical evaluations require each student to conduct a fully evaluated PRT session, demonstrating proper exercise selection, cueing, and safety standards. Students must also design a complete unit fitness program that addresses mission-specific physical demands and presents a progressive training plan.
