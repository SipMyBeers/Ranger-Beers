The Exercise Science phase builds the academic foundation every Master Fitness Trainer needs. Students learn human anatomy, physiology, and kinesiology — the science of how the body moves, adapts, and responds to physical stress.

Nutrition science covers macronutrient balance, hydration strategies, and performance fueling. Injury prevention training teaches students to identify risk factors, recognize overtraining symptoms, and implement corrective strategies before soldiers get hurt.

Written examinations test comprehension of exercise science fundamentals. Students who cannot demonstrate mastery of the scientific principles will not advance to Program Design.

Exercise Science Topics
  • Human anatomy — musculoskeletal, cardiovascular, respiratory systems
  • Exercise physiology — energy systems, muscle fiber types, adaptation
  • Kinesiology — joint mechanics, planes of movement, biomechanics
  • Nutrition — macronutrients, hydration, pre/post-workout fueling
  • Injury prevention — overuse, acute injuries, corrective exercise
  • Principles of training — overload, specificity, periodization
  • ACFT event analysis — physical demands by event

Program Design is where the science becomes practical. Students apply everything from Phase 1 to build real physical readiness training programs tailored to specific unit missions and individual soldier needs.

Each student must conduct a fully evaluated PRT session, demonstrating proper exercise selection, cueing, safety standards, and the ability to coach soldiers through correct movement patterns. Teaching ability matters as much as knowledge.

The capstone requires designing a complete unit fitness program that addresses mission-specific physical demands, presents a progressive and periodized training plan, and accounts for injury prevention and recovery.

Program Design Events
  • Periodization — building progressive training cycles
  • Unit PRT program design — mission-specific programming
  • ACFT-focused training plans — event-specific preparation
  • Evaluated PRT session — lead a full training session
  • Individual fitness assessment — evaluate and prescribe
  • Corrective exercise programming — address weaknesses
  • Teaching methodology — cueing, demonstration, coaching

Don't Be That MFT Student.

Every failure at MFT has a type. We built a fix for each one.

🧠
No Exercise Science?

Anatomy, physiology, and kinesiology are the backbone of the written exam. If you can't name the muscles or explain energy systems, you won't pass Phase 1.
📋
Can't Design Programs?

Periodization, progressive overload, and mission-specific programming are all tested. You need to build a complete unit PRT plan from scratch.
📣
Bad at Teaching PT?

Your evaluated PRT session is pass/fail. If you can't cue exercises, command a formation, and coach proper movement patterns, you're done.
🧬
Don't Know Anatomy?

Muscles, joints, planes of movement, and biomechanics are foundational. Study anatomy before you arrive or you'll spend the entire course catching up.
🏋
Weak ACFT Coaching?

MFTs are the unit's ACFT experts. If you can't analyze each event, identify weaknesses, and prescribe corrective training, your soldiers will suffer.
🩹
No Injury Prevention?

Overuse injuries destroy readiness. MFTs must identify risk factors, implement prehab, and modify training before soldiers break down.
