Course Overview

What is Airborne School?

The U.S. Army Basic Airborne Course (BAC) is conducted at Fort Moore (formerly Fort Benning), Georgia, by the 1st Battalion (Airborne), 507th Infantry Regiment. The three-week course trains soldiers, sailors, airmen, and Marines in military parachuting. Graduates are awarded the Basic Parachutist Badge — commonly known as "jump wings."

Airborne School is one of the most iconic courses in the U.S. military. It transforms ground soldiers into paratroopers capable of conducting forced-entry operations via static-line parachute. The course emphasizes discipline, physical fitness, and mastery of parachute landing falls (PLFs).

Course Phases

3 Weeks to Jump Wings

  • Week 1 Ground Week — Students learn the fundamentals of parachuting: proper body position, parachute landing falls (PLFs), aircraft procedures, and emergency drills. Physical training includes runs, pull-ups, and the lateral drift apparatus.
  • Week 2 Tower Week — Training progresses to the 34-foot tower for mock-door exits and the 250-foot free-drop towers. Students practice mass exits, wind drift, and equipment rigging while continuing intense PT.
  • Week 3 Jump Week — Students execute five live parachute jumps from a C-130 or C-17 aircraft at 1,250 feet AGL. At least one jump is conducted with combat equipment. Successful completion of all five jumps earns the Basic Parachutist Badge.
Key Standards

What It Takes

Candidates must pass a fitness assessment including a timed two-mile run, push-ups, sit-ups, and pull-ups. Students must demonstrate mastery of PLFs, aircraft procedures, and emergency protocols before progressing to each phase. Any refusal to jump results in immediate dismissal from the course.

The physical demands are moderate compared to other Army schools, but the mental challenge of stepping out of a perfectly good aircraft at 1,250 feet is the real test. Zero tolerance for safety violations keeps the graduation rate high but the standards absolute.
