Jumpmaster Standards

Prerequisites, course standards, and grading criteria for the Jumpmaster Course.

Prerequisites

Entry Requirements

The Jumpmaster Course requires candidates to be airborne-qualified with significant jump experience. This is not an entry-level course — students must arrive with a strong foundation in parachute operations and demonstrate readiness for advanced airborne responsibilities.

Course Requirements

What You Must Demonstrate

  • Pass all written examinations with minimum 70% score
  • Complete the 18-point JMPI sequence within time standard with zero missed major deficiencies
  • Correctly identify all deliberate deficiencies on rigged jumpers during practical evaluations
  • Demonstrate proper aircraft procedures and jumpmaster commands
  • Conduct drop zone reconnaissance and wind drift calculations
  • Serve as jumpmaster on live airborne operations including day and night jumps
Grading

How You're Evaluated

The Jumpmaster Course uses a combination of written exams and practical evaluations. Written exams require a minimum 70% to pass. Practical JMPI evaluations are graded on the ability to detect all major deficiencies within the time standard — there is zero tolerance for missed deficiencies that could endanger a jumper.

The Practical Phase (JMPI) eliminates the most candidates. Students inspect rigged jumpers with deliberate faults inserted. Missing a major deficiency or false-calling a properly rigged jumper results in a NO-GO. Accumulated NO-GOs lead to course dismissal with no recycle option for integrity violations.
